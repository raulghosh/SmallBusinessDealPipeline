### What They Are Likely Not Telling You

1. **Revenue seasonality and weather impact:** Chattanooga TN gets meaningful rainfall. Express wash revenue can swing 30-50% between best and worst months. Has the seller presented annualized figures based on peak months? Demand 36 months of monthly revenue data.

2. **Membership quality:** 450 "active" members -- but what is the churn rate? If monthly churn is 8-10%, the membership base is a revolving door, not a stable asset. What is the average membership tenure? What tier are members on (basic vs. premium)?

3. **Why "relocating"?** People relocate for many reasons. But a business owner who relocates after only 6 years of operating a $680K revenue car wash is unusual. Did a spouse get a job transfer? Is the market not performing as expected? Is there a new competitor coming? The brevity of ownership combined with the non-retirement exit deserves scrutiny.

4. **Equipment condition behind the "installed 2020" claim:** The tunnel was installed 6 years ago. At this age, the conveyor chain may be approaching its 5-7 year replacement window ($80K-$200K). Brushes/cloth need annual replacement. Has this been done? Request maintenance logs.

5. **Traffic count verification:** The listing does not provide a traffic count. For an express tunnel, 25,000+ cars/day past the site is minimum; 40,000+ is ideal. If the traffic count is not disclosed, it may be below threshold.

### Add-Back Red Flags

No add-back schedule has been disclosed. The $240,000 SDE figure is unverified. Without a line-by-line add-back breakdown, treat this number as aspirational until tax returns and P&L statements confirm it. At 35.3% SDE margin, the number is within the plausible range for express tunnels (25-40%), but verification is essential.

### The "Relocating" Exit Reason -- Decoded

"Relocating out of state" is a hard reason to disprove but also a hard reason to trust. Genuine relocators typically have a specific destination and reason (spouse's job, family obligations, new business in another state). Sellers who use "relocating" as a cover may actually be:
- Burned out after 6 years and dressing it up as a life event
- Seeing revenue plateau or decline and wanting to exit before it gets worse
- Aware of a competitive threat (new car wash construction nearby)
- Needing liquidity for something they do not want to disclose

The refusal to offer any seller financing makes this worse. A genuinely relocating seller who believes in the business would typically accept a 10-15% seller note for tax benefits alone. Refusing all financing while also relocating signals: "I want all my money now and I want to be completely disconnected from this business." That is the posture of someone who expects the business to underperform after the sale.

## 9. DUE DILIGENCE PRIORITY QUESTIONS

**1. "Can we request the card system transaction logs directly from the payment processor for the last 24 months? We'd like to verify revenue independently."**

Good answer: Fixed escalation (2-3%/year) or CPI-based. Bad answer: Market-rate reset at renewal (could double rent).

Good answer: Seller provides actual bills. Bad answer: "I can tell you approximately what they are" (estimates are not verification).

**5. "What does a typical week look like for you as the owner? How many hours do you spend on the business, and what do those hours consist of?"**
Good answer: "I stop by once a week for about an hour to check on things and make sure everything is clean." Bad answer: "I'm there most days" (would contradict the unattended claim).

**6. "What is the current rent, and what percentage of revenue does it represent?"**
Good answer: Rent at or below 22% of revenue. Bad answer: Rent above 25% of revenue (structural profitability concern) or seller is vague.

**7. "What is the maintenance arrangement for the machines? Is there a service contract, or do you handle repairs yourself?"**
Good answer: Service contract with Speed Queen/Dexter or a local commercial laundry tech. Bad answer: "I do most of the repairs myself" (that labor must be replaced post-acquisition, adding cost).

**8. "Is there any coin revenue in addition to the card system, and if so, what percentage of total revenue is coin vs. card?"**
Good answer: "95%+ is on the card system." Bad answer: "About half is still coins" (the coin portion is unverifiable).

**9. "What prompted you to install the card system in 2021?"**
Good answer: "Customers were asking for it" or "I wanted to track revenue better for the eventual sale." Bad answer: Evasiveness or "the old system broke" (suggests reactive, not strategic).

**10. "Are there any other laundromats within 2 miles? Has a new one opened recently?"**
Good answer: "There's one about 3 miles away, no new ones." Bad answer: "A new one opened last year" (competitive pressure).

**11. "What is the renter percentage within 1 mile of the location, and has the neighborhood been changing?"**
Good answer: High renter percentage (>40%), stable or growing. Bad answer: Area is gentrifying with new condos replacing apartments.
