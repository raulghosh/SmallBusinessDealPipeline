#### 9. DUE DILIGENCE PRIORITY QUESTIONS

1. **"Can you confirm the business is not a franchise? This is a threshold requirement for us."**
   - Good answer: "Confirmed independent, no franchise affiliation." Bad answer: Any hedging, brand licensing mention, or franchise fee structure.

2. **"What is the gross revenue for the trailing twelve months, and can you share revenue for the prior two years as well?"**
   - Good answer: Specific dollar figures with trend data. Bad answer: "We can share that under NDA" (for a basic figure like revenue range) or a round number estimate.

3. **"How long has the business been operating, and has it changed ownership at any point?"**
   - Good answer: "Established in [year], original/second owner." Bad answer: Vague response or "I'd need to check."

4. **"Does the business currently have a general manager or operations manager on payroll? If so, what is their tenure and compensation?"**
   - Good answer: Named individual with 2+ years tenure and stated salary. Bad answer: "The owner handles things, but it's only a few hours a week."

5. **"What percentage of revenue comes from the top 5 clients? Are there any clients representing more than 15% of revenue?"**
   - Good answer: Specific percentages, all under 15%. Bad answer: "We have a diversified client base" without numbers, or deflection to post-LOI disclosure.

6. **"Are client contracts written and multi-year, or month-to-month? What's the typical contract length and termination clause?"**
   - Good answer: "Most are 2-3 year written contracts with 90-day termination clauses." Bad answer: "We have strong relationships" (code for verbal agreements).

7. **"What does the current owner's typical work week look like? Specifically, what tasks occupy their time?"**
   - Good answer: "The owner focuses on strategic growth; day-to-day is handled by the team." Bad answer: "The owner is very involved but it's manageable" (code for owner-dependent).

8. **"Why is the owner selling? How long has the business been on the market?"**
   - Good answer: Clear, simple answer (genuine retirement with timeline). Bad answer: Elaborate multi-reason explanation or vague "exploring options."

9. **"Would the seller be open to seller financing for 15-20% of the purchase price?"**
   - Good answer: "Yes, the seller would consider a note." Bad answer: "The seller wants all cash at close" (especially concerning for a retirement sale).

10. **"What does the equipment fleet look like -- number of vehicles, age, and mileage? Is there a recent equipment inventory?"**
    - Good answer: Itemized list with ages and conditions. Bad answer: A single dollar figure for FF&E without breakdown.

11. **"How many W-2s were issued in each of the last two years, and what is the current headcount?"**
    - Good answer: Consistent numbers suggesting low turnover. Bad answer: Large gap between W-2 count and current headcount (signals high turnover).

12. **"Can you share the detailed SDE add-back schedule? We'd like to see each add-back line item."**
    - Good answer: Itemized schedule provided promptly. Bad answer: "The add-backs are standard" or "we can share that in due diligence."

#### 9. DUE DILIGENCE PRIORITY QUESTIONS

(To be asked if/when the current LOI expires or the deal becomes available)

1. **"What are the trailing twelve-month revenue and SDE figures? Can you share 3 years of P&L and tax returns?"**
   - Good answer: Specific figures provided promptly with supporting documentation. Bad answer: Vague ranges or further delay.

   - Good answer: Well-diversified, no client over 15%, multi-year written contracts. Bad answer: Heavy concentration or verbal agreements.

   - Good answer: Clear succession story with planned timeline. Bad answer: Vague or conflicting narratives.

   - Good answer: "Yes, the seller is open to a note." Bad answer: "All cash at close" (especially unusual for a retirement/succession sale).

   - Good answer: Detailed list showing equipment with meaningful life remaining. Bad answer: "FF&E is valued at $186,746" with no breakdown (could include depreciated vehicles and worn equipment).

9. **"How many employees are there currently, and how many W-2s were issued in each of the last two years?"**
   - Good answer: Stable headcount, low turnover. Bad answer: Significant gap between W-2 count and current staff.

10. **"If an earnout tied to 12-month client retention were part of the deal structure, would the seller be open to that?"**
    - Good answer: "Yes, the seller is confident clients will stay." Bad answer: Seller recoils or insists on all-cash (may know clients are at risk).
