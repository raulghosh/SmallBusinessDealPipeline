# Diligence Question Bank

A curated set of buyer-side diligence questions organized by category. Use these in the
DRAFT_INITIAL_OUTREACH (for initial deal-specific questions), Post-CIM Questions emails
(Template 3), and the SATISFACTION_CHECK mode.

**Usage rules:**
- Never send more than 10 questions in a single email
- For Post-CIM Questions, pick 3-4 from Financials, 2-3 from Operations, 2-3 from
  Customers/Contracts, and 1-2 from Lease — then add 1-2 vertical-specific questions
- Prioritize unanswered questions from earlier rounds before adding new categories
- Always personalize question language with deal-specific numbers from the CIM
  (e.g., "the CIM states $340K SDE — can you confirm the specific add-backs?")

---

## Category 1: Financials

*Pick 3-4 max per email. Always prioritize SDE verification first.*

**F1.** What are the TTM (trailing twelve months) revenue and SDE figures as of [current date],
and are these consistent with the numbers in the CIM?

**F2.** Can you provide the last 3 years of P&L statements and the corresponding business
tax returns (Schedule C or Form 1120S)? We want to confirm the CIM figures reconcile with
tax-reported income.

**F3.** The CIM states SDE of $[X]. Can you walk me through the specific add-backs that bridge
from net income to that figure? (We want to understand each add-back line item individually.)

**F4.** Are there any deferred revenues, customer deposits, or off-balance-sheet liabilities
that a buyer would inherit post-closing?

**F5.** What is the current accounts receivable aging? Are there any outstanding balances
over 90 days, and how have those been handled historically?

**F6.** Are there any outstanding loans, lines of credit, or equipment financing the business
currently carries? What is the total outstanding debt?

**F7.** Has revenue trended up, flat, or down over the last 3 years? What is the primary
driver of that trend?

**F8.** Are there any one-time revenues or expenses in the financials that are not recurring
and that a buyer should know about?

---

## Category 2: Operations

*Pick 2-3 max per email. Management and owner-dependence are the top priorities for absentee buyers.*

**O1.** How many employees are there in total, and what are their roles and approximate tenure?
Is anyone on a formal employment contract?

**O2.** Is there a manager or supervisor in place who handles day-to-day operations and would
be willing to stay on post-sale?

**O3.** What does the owner do on a typical workday? How many hours per week does the owner
actively work in the business?

**O4.** What systems and software does the business use for scheduling, billing, customer
communication, and routing? (Examples: Jobber, Service Titan, QuickBooks, proprietary systems)

**O5.** How long has the current management team (including any lead employees) been in place?
Have there been any significant staffing changes in the last 12 months?

**O6.** Is there a documented operations manual, training materials, or SOP documentation
that a new owner could rely on?

**O7.** Are there any key vendor or supplier relationships that are personal to the current
owner and might be at risk post-sale?

---

## Category 3: Customers and Contracts

*Pick 2-3 max per email. Customer concentration is the most critical risk for service businesses.*

**C1.** Who are the top 5 customers by revenue, and what percentage of total revenue does each
represent? Is any single customer more than 20% of total revenue?

**C2.** Are customer relationships governed by written contracts or verbal agreements?
What are the typical contract terms (length, notice period, auto-renewal provisions)?

**C3.** What is the average customer tenure? How many customers has the business retained
for more than 3 years?

**C4.** Have any major customers given notice of cancellation, reduced service, or expressed
concerns in the last 12 months?

**C5.** Are any of the key customer relationships personal to the current owner — in other
words, is there any customer who chose the business because of a personal relationship with
the seller that may not transfer to a new owner?

**C6.** What is the customer churn rate on an annual basis? How does the business typically
replace lost customers (referrals, marketing, outbound sales)?

**C7.** Is there a signed customer list with contact information that would be transferred as
part of the sale?

---

## Category 4: Assets and Equipment

*Pick 1-2 max per email. Focus on what's included and what's at end of life.*

**A1.** What equipment and vehicles are included in the sale? Is any of it currently financed
or under a lease obligation that would transfer to the buyer?

**A2.** When was the major equipment last serviced or replaced? Do maintenance records exist
and would they be available for review during due diligence?

**A3.** Are there any known deferred maintenance items — equipment that should be repaired or
replaced in the near term — that a buyer would be inheriting?

**A4.** Is there a current equipment appraisal or depreciation schedule available?

---

## Category 5: Lease and Real Estate

*Pick 1-2 max per email. Lease terms are deal-critical for absentee buyers.*

**L1.** What are the current lease terms — specifically: monthly rent, lease expiration date,
renewal options, and CAM (Common Area Maintenance) charges if applicable?

**L2.** Does the lease include a clause allowing assignment to a new owner upon sale?
Is the landlord aware of the sale, and has the seller obtained any preliminary consent?

**L3.** Is the current owner also the property owner (landlord)? If so, will the real estate
be sold separately, and what are the proposed lease terms from the seller-as-landlord?

**L4.** Has the landlord made any recent statements about rent increases or lease renewal terms
that could materially affect the business's cost structure post-sale?

---

## Category 6: Legal and Compliance

*Pick 1-2 max per email. Flag anything that could block a clean title transfer.*

**LG1.** Are there any pending or historical lawsuits, liens, judgments, or regulatory
actions against the business or the current owner in their capacity as business operator?

**LG2.** Are all required business licenses, permits, and certifications current and in good
standing? Are they transferable to a new owner upon sale, or would the buyer need to reapply?

**LG3.** Are there any ongoing regulatory requirements or compliance obligations the buyer
would inherit (e.g., OSHA requirements, EPA permits, city business licenses)?

## Vertical-Specific Questions: Janitorial / Commercial Cleaning

*Use for all janitorial deals. Add to Category 3 questions on customers/contracts.*

**J1.** What is the average contract length for commercial cleaning agreements, and what is
the historical renewal rate at the end of each contract term?

**J2.** How many active contracts does the business currently hold? Is there significant
concentration — for example, does one contract represent more than 25% of revenue?

**J3.** What percentage of revenue comes from commercial clients vs. residential clients?
(Commercial is preferred for stability and scalability; residential is higher churn.)

**J4.** Does the business hold any multi-year contracts with anchor clients? If so, what are
the notice and exit provisions?

**J5.** Are there any government, municipal, or institutional contracts in the portfolio?
If so, what are the bid/renewal requirements and do they transfer automatically?

**J6.** Does the business currently compete for new contracts through a formal sales process,
or does it rely primarily on referrals? What does the pipeline look like?

---

## Vertical-Specific Questions: Car Wash

*Use for all car wash deals. Focus on membership economics and equipment state.*

**CW1.** How many active monthly unlimited wash memberships does the business currently carry,
and what is the average monthly membership fee?

**CW2.** What is the annual churn rate on memberships — meaning what percentage of members
cancel each month or each year?

**CW3.** What is the current revenue mix between memberships vs. single-wash transactions?
What percentage of total revenue comes from each?

**CW4.** What type of equipment is in use (manufacturer, model, year installed)? Has there
been a major equipment overhaul or replacement in the last 5 years?

**CW5.** Does the facility have a water reclaim/recycling system in place? What are the
current water costs per month, and are there any open environmental permits or compliance
requirements?

**CW6.** What is the property lease structure? Is the land owned or leased, and if leased,
what are the remaining term and renewal options? (Car wash economics are extremely
lease-sensitive given the fixed equipment investment.)

---

## Vertical-Specific Questions: Laundromat

*Use for all laundromat deals. Revenue verification and equipment age are the core issues.*

**LM1.** What is the current revenue mix between coin-operated, card-operated, and app-based
payment methods? What percentage of total revenue does each represent?

**LM2.** When were the washing machines and dryers last replaced? What are the brands and
models currently in use, and how many of each are in service?

**LM3.** Does the business offer any value-added services such as WDF (wash-dry-fold), pickup
and delivery, or commercial laundry accounts? What percentage of revenue comes from these?

**LM4.** What is the current utility cost structure (water, gas, electric) per month, and how
has it trended over the past 3 years?

**LM5.** Are there any outstanding equipment leases or financing arrangements on the machines
that would transfer to a new buyer?

**LM6.** How is revenue tracked and reported? Are there digital vending records, or does the
business rely primarily on physical coin counting? (Revenue verification methodology is a
due diligence critical point for laundromats.)

---

## Question Priority Matrix

When constructing a question email, prioritize in this order:

1. **SDE verification** (Category 1, F3) — always first if SDE has not been confirmed with
   add-back detail
2. **Owner dependence / manager in place** (Category 2, O2 and O3) — critical for absentee
3. **Customer concentration** (Category 3, C1) — single-customer risk can be a deal-killer
4. **Lease terms** (Category 5, L1 and L2) — lease issues are non-negotiable blockers
5. **Vertical-specific top question** — first question in each vertical section above
6. **Everything else** — fill remaining slots from other categories

**Questions you should never need to ask (because they should be in the CIM):**
- What type of business is this?
- Where is the business located?
- What is the asking price?
- How long has it been in business?

If these aren't in the CIM, the CIM is incomplete — note this as a red flag rather than
asking basic questions, as it reflects on the quality of the broker's preparation.
