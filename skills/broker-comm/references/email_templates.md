# Email Templates — Broker Communication

All templates are for buyer-side use only. Personalize every template with deal-specific
details before use. Generic emails get deprioritized by brokers.

Tone across all templates: **professional, direct, concise.** Brokers are busy and deal with
tire-kickers constantly. Every email should signal that this buyer is serious, qualified, and
efficient with everyone's time.

---

## Template 1: Initial Inquiry

**When to use:** First contact with a broker on a specific listing. Sent when deal moves from
SHORTLISTED to CONTACTING.

**[SUBJECT]:** Acquisition Inquiry — [Business Type], [City/State] — [Listing ID or Brief Descriptor]

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

I came across your listing for [brief business descriptor — e.g., "the commercial janitorial
business in Charlotte, NC"] on [source — e.g., BizBuySell]. I'm actively looking to acquire
a profitable [janitorial / car wash / laundromat] business in the Southeast and this one fits
my criteria well.

Quick background: I'm a cash-and-financing buyer targeting businesses in the $[X]–$[X] range.
I've completed due diligence on several businesses in this space and move quickly when the
fundamentals are solid.

A few questions before I request the CIM:
- Is the $[asking price] firm, or is the seller open to discussion based on verified
  financials?
- Is there an existing manager or key employee who would remain post-sale?
- Are the financial statements (P&L + tax returns) available with a signed NDA?

Happy to sign your NDA. Could you send it over along with any available financials or a
CIM?

Best regards,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Use the exact business type from the listing (janitorial, commercial cleaning, car wash,
  laundromat — not a generic term)
- Reference the specific listing source (BizBuySell, BizQuest, LoopNet, broker website)
- Insert actual asking price from the listing
- Insert buyer's revenue range if it matches the deal — shows qualification without
  overcommitting
- Mention city/state to show geographic intent is real

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Visa status, H1B, immigration history, or work authorization of any kind
- Internal deal ID or deal score from the evaluation memo
- Any statement suggesting this is the buyer's first acquisition
- Questions about why the seller is selling (too early — reads as suspicious)
- Price negotiation or lowball signals in the first email
- Lengthy backgrounds or investment thesis — keep it under 200 words

## Template 2: NDA Follow-Up

**When to use:** Broker was contacted (Template 1 sent) but has not responded with NDA or CIM
within 5 business days. Light nudge — not urgent.

**[SUBJECT]:** Re: Acquisition Inquiry — [Business Type], [City/State]

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

Just following up on my note from [date of original email] regarding the [business type]
listing.

If the listing is still active and the NDA is ready, I'm happy to sign and move forward
quickly.

Let me know if there's a better way to connect — happy to do a quick call if that's easier.

Best,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Reference the exact date the first email was sent (shows the buyer is organized)
- Offer a call — some brokers prefer voice for initial qualification

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Pressure language ("I need to hear back by...")
- Multiple follow-up questions — that was covered in the first email
- Any hint of frustration or impatience
- Visa/H1B status

## Template 3: Post-CIM Questions

**When to use:** After receiving the CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum). Sent in
IN_DILIGENCE phase to begin structured diligence. Pull 6-8 targeted questions from
`diligence_question_bank.md` — do not exceed 10 questions per email.

**[SUBJECT]:** [Business Type] Acquisition — Diligence Questions (CIM Review)

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

Thanks for sending the CIM. I've reviewed it carefully and have a focused set of questions
before moving further.

**Financials:**
1. [Question from question bank — e.g., "Can you confirm the TTM SDE as of [date] and walk
   me through the specific add-backs?"]
2. [Question from question bank]
3. [Question from question bank]

**Operations:**
4. [Question from question bank]
5. [Question from question bank]

**Customers/Contracts:**
6. [Question from question bank]

**Lease:**
7. [Question from question bank]

I'm trying to move quickly — if these can be addressed in a call, I'm available [specific
day/time window] or happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Reference specific numbers from the CIM (e.g., "The CIM shows $340K SDE — I'd like to
  confirm the add-backs that bring it to that figure")
- Choose questions relevant to the specific vertical (see diligence_question_bank.md for
  janitorial, car wash, laundromat specific questions)
- Offer a call as an alternative to written Q&A — brokers often prefer calls for complex
  questions
- Include specific availability to signal urgency

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- More than 10 questions per email
- Questions about the seller's personal motivation for selling (too early)
- Negative comments about the CIM quality or what's missing
- Price discussion
- Visa/H1B status

## Template 4: Serious Interest Letter

**When to use:** After key diligence questions have been answered satisfactorily. This is NOT
an LOI. It signals to the broker that the buyer is moving toward an offer and requests a
seller intro call. Sent when transitioning from IN_DILIGENCE to READY_TO_MEET.

**[SUBJECT]:** [Business Type] Acquisition — Moving Forward / Intro Call Request

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

After reviewing the financials and the answers to my questions, I'm impressed with what I've
seen. The business aligns well with my acquisition criteria.

I'd like to move to the next step — an introductory call with the seller to discuss the
business and the transition. I'm also beginning to work with my financing partner in parallel.

Are you able to arrange a 30-minute intro call this week or next? I'm flexible on timing and
can work around the seller's schedule.

Before the call, it would be helpful to have:
- [Remaining open question, if any — e.g., "The last 12 months of bank statements"]
- [Remaining open question, if any]

Looking forward to getting this moving.

Best,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Mention 1-2 specific things from the diligence that impressed — shows genuine engagement
  (e.g., "The contract renewal rate is strong" or "The management structure looks solid")
- Only list remaining open items if truly necessary — this email should signal confidence,
  not hesitation
- Reference financing to show the buyer is a real buyer (not speculation)

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Price discussion or offer hints
- Any statement that could be interpreted as a conditional LOI
- Overenthusiasm that weakens negotiating position
- Visa/H1B status

## Template 5: Request for Seller Meeting

**When to use:** Broker has confirmed seller is willing to meet. Confirming logistics for the
intro call (seller + broker + buyer). This is the final step before LOI consideration.

**[SUBJECT]:** Re: [Business Type] Acquisition — Call Confirmation

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

Looking forward to the call with [seller name / "the seller"]. To confirm:

- Date/Time: [confirmed date and time]
- Format: [phone / Zoom / in-person]
- Dial-in / link: [if applicable]

A few topics I'd like to cover on the call:
- Operational overview and management structure
- Transition expectations and seller's preferred timeline
- Any open items from the CIM review

If there's anything specific the seller would like me to prepare or review beforehand, happy
to do that.

Best,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Use the seller's first name if it has been provided at this stage
- List 2-3 topics specific to the deal (not generic) — signals the buyer has done their homework
- Keep it short — the real conversation happens on the call

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Price anchors or negotiation language
- Questions that should have been asked earlier (shows the buyer wasn't paying attention)
- Visa/H1B status

## Template 6: Soft Pass

**When to use:** Buyer decides to pass on a deal — but wants to keep the relationship with
the broker open for future deals. Use when the deal didn't work out for neutral reasons
(valuation, geography, fit) rather than broker fault.

**[SUBJECT]:** Re: [Business Type] Listing — Stepping Back

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

Thank you for your time and for sharing the materials on [business type / brief descriptor].
After a thorough review, I've decided not to move forward on this particular deal — the fit
isn't quite right for my current criteria.

This isn't a reflection on the business or your representation. I'm continuing to look
actively in the [janitorial / car wash / laundromat] space across the Southeast and would
welcome your help on future listings that come to market.

If you have other businesses in the [revenue range] range, please keep me on your list.

Best regards,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Be specific enough about the reason to be credible, but vague enough to protect negotiating
  position (never say "the price was too high" — say "the fit isn't right")
- Reference the specific vertical and region to keep the door open intelligently
- Reference revenue range to help the broker remember what the buyer is looking for

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Specific criticisms of the business or the seller
- Price feedback (e.g., "the asking price was too high") — this kills the relationship
- Detailed explanations — brief is professional
- Any commitment to look at future deals ("please keep me on your list" is sufficient)
- Visa/H1B status

## Template 7: Hard Pass

**When to use:** Broker has been evasive, wasted significant time, been dishonest, or failed
to deliver on commitments (e.g., promised CIM multiple times and never delivered). Professional
but final. Does NOT keep the door open.

**[SUBJECT]:** [Business Type] Listing — Closing Out

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

I'm going to close out my interest in this listing. I've invested significant time in the
review process and unfortunately haven't been able to get the information needed to move
forward.

I wish you and your seller the best of luck.

[Buyer Name]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- None needed. Keep it brief. Brevity sends its own message.

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Specific accusations or grievances — these can backfire professionally
- Requests for future deal flow (they won't be helpful)
- Long explanations
- Visa/H1B status

**[TONE NOTES]:** Cool, professional, short. The goal is to end the relationship cleanly
without leaving anything on the table or creating any liability.

## Template 8: Follow-Up Nudge

**When to use:** Generic follow-up when the broker has gone quiet during IN_DILIGENCE or
CONTACTING and no more specific template applies. Minimum 5 business days since last contact.

**[SUBJECT]:** Re: [Original Subject Line]

**[BODY]:**
```
Hi [Broker First Name],

Just checking in on this one — still very much interested and happy to keep things moving
quickly on my end. Let me know if there's anything you need from me or if there's been an
update on the seller's side.

Best,
[Buyer Name]
[Phone Number]
```

**[PERSONALIZATION]:**
- Use the exact original subject line with "Re:" prefix — keep it in the thread
- Can add one specific reference to context (e.g., "I'm still working through the CIM and
  have the questions ready to send once I hear back")

**[DO NOT INCLUDE]:**
- Frustration or pressure ("I've been waiting for two weeks...")
- Multiple questions — the nudge is not the place for new asks
- Ultimatums or deadlines
- Visa/H1B status

---

## Template Selection Guide

| Situation | Template |
|-----------|----------|
| First email to broker on a new deal | Template 1: Initial Inquiry |
| No reply after 5 business days | Template 2: NDA Follow-Up |
| CIM received, starting diligence | Template 3: Post-CIM Questions |
| Diligence mostly done, want to meet | Template 4: Serious Interest Letter |
| Seller call confirmed, confirming logistics | Template 5: Request for Seller Meeting |
| Buyer decides to pass, good broker | Template 6: Soft Pass |
| Broker was difficult / wasted time | Template 7: Hard Pass |
| Broker went quiet mid-process | Template 8: Follow-Up Nudge |
