# Valuation Benchmarks — Janitorial & Commercial Cleaning Businesses

Sources: BizBuySell Cleaning & Janitorial Valuation Report, Peak Business Valuation,
IBISWorld SIC 7349, OffDeal, Sigma Mergers, ValuAdder, CleanLink

---

## STANDARD VALUATION MULTIPLES

### SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) Multiples
For small to mid-market cleaning businesses ($500K–$5M revenue):

| Multiple Range | What It Signals |
|---|---|
| Below 2.0x SDE | Distressed sale, something is wrong, or seller is extremely motivated |
| 2.0x–2.5x SDE | Discount range — older business, high concentration risk, or owner-dependent |
| **2.5x–3.0x SDE** | **Fair market — typical for stable, recurring-revenue commercial cleaning** |
| **3.0x–3.5x SDE** | **Premium — warranted by long-term contracts, strong management, low concentration** |
| 3.5x–4.0x SDE | Rich — requires written multi-year contracts and verified absentee operations |
| Above 4.0x SDE | Very expensive — only justified by large creditworthy client base and strong growth |

**Rule of thumb for negotiation:** Start at 2.5x verified SDE. Move toward 3.0x if long-term
written contracts are confirmed. Move toward 3.5x+ only if the business is fully systematized
with a proven GM in place and zero client concentration risk.

### EBITDA Multiples
For larger operations where EBITDA differs meaningfully from SDE:

| Multiple Range | Context |
|---|---|
| 3.0x–3.5x EBITDA | Small market, owner-operated |
| **3.5x–4.5x EBITDA** | **Mid-market, good systems, recurring revenue** |
| 4.5x–6.0x EBITDA | Institutional-quality, multi-location, long-term government/healthcare contracts |

### Revenue Multiples
Less commonly used as primary valuation, but useful as a secondary check:

| Multiple Range | Context |
|---|---|
| 0.3x–0.5x Revenue | Commodity cleaning, low margin, high labor intensity |
| **0.5x–0.8x Revenue** | **Typical range for well-run independent commercial cleaning** |
| 0.8x–1.2x Revenue | Strong specialty niche (medical, industrial, restoration, government) |
| Above 1.2x Revenue | Rare — usually reflects proprietary contracts or market position |

## WHAT DRIVES VALUATION PREMIUMS (AND BY HOW MUCH)

These factors can justify moving toward the higher end of the SDE multiple range:

| Factor | Typical Multiple Lift | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-year written contracts (2+ years) | +0.3x–0.5x | Must be transferable — verify assignment clauses |
| Zero client concentration (no client >15% of revenue) | +0.2x–0.4x | Demand revenue breakdown by client |
| Full-time GM running operations (proven, salaried) | +0.3x–0.5x | Most important for absentee buyers |
| 15+ year operating history | +0.1x–0.3x | Proven cycle survival |
| Specialty niche (medical, biohazard, industrial) | +0.2x–0.5x | Higher barriers to entry, pricing power |
| Consistent revenue growth 3+ years | +0.2x–0.4x | Must be organic, not just new client addition |
| SBA pre-approval or lender engagement | +0.1x | Third-party validation of financials |
| Real estate included or below-market lease | +0.1x–0.2x | Reduces operational risk |

## WHAT DRIVES VALUATION DISCOUNTS

These factors should be used to push price toward the lower end of the range or justify a pass:

| Factor | Discount Signal | Your Move |
|---|---|---|
| Business less than 5 years old | Unproven through economic cycle | Push below 2.5x or pass |
| Any single client >30% of revenue | Concentration = existential risk | Demand escrow or earnout protection |
| Owner works 40+ hrs/week in operations | Absentee not possible without re-hiring | Price in cost of GM ($60–90K/yr) |
| Month-to-month verbal contracts | "Recurring revenue" is marketing, not fact | Push to 2.0x–2.5x |
| Revenue declined in any of last 3 years | Ask why before any offer | Distressed pricing required |
| No seller financing offered | Reduced seller confidence or over-leverage | Higher risk premium, lower offer |
| Price reductions on current listing | Sat on market = market rejected prior price | Start below most recent ask |
| Established year unknown or withheld | Hiding something about founding history | Flag as moderate red flag |
| Relocation possible (listed as "relocatable") | Business has no geographic stickiness | Clients may not follow new owner |
| High equipment age / FF&E heavily depreciated | Cap-ex replacement cost likely in year 1–3 | Adjust offer down by estimated replacement cost |

## MARKET BENCHMARKS (INDUSTRY CONTEXT)

### U.S. Janitorial Services Market
- Total market size: ~$112 billion (2026, IBISWorld)
- CAGR: 3.3% projected through 2033
- Number of businesses: ~870,000 (extremely fragmented)
- 95% of companies are micro or small businesses under $5M revenue
- Top 4 players (ABM, ServiceMaster, etc.) hold ~15% of market — the rest is fragmented independents

### Typical Financial Profile of a $500K–$2M Revenue Independent
- Gross margin: 40–55% (after direct labor and supplies)
- Net margin (before owner comp): 10–20%
- Owner's SDE: typically 15–25% of gross revenue
- Labor as % of revenue: 45–60% (the biggest cost driver)
- Recurring contract revenue as % of total: highly variable (20–95%)

### Regional Pricing Note (Southeast U.S.)
Businesses in GA, TN, NC, SC, AL tend to trade at the lower end of national multiples
due to lower labor costs but also higher labor availability (more supply of cleaning workers).
This is a slight advantage for buyers relative to coastal markets.

## SANITY CHECK CALCULATIONS

### Is the SDE Plausible?
For a cleaning business:
- SDE should be ~15–25% of gross revenue
- If SDE is above 30% of revenue: verify — either margins are exceptional or add-backs are aggressive
- If SDE is below 10% of revenue: business may be underperforming or overstaffed

**Example check:** Revenue $1,346,277 → Expected SDE range: $201,941–$336,569
- Reported SDE $227,898 → ~17% of revenue → plausible and conservative

### Is the Revenue Multiple Consistent with the SDE Multiple?
If revenue multiple and SDE multiple tell very different stories, ask why:
- Low revenue multiple but high SDE multiple → thin margins baked into price, OR add-backs are inflated
- High revenue multiple but low SDE multiple → business valued on topline, margins are poor
- Both multiples in fair range → consistent and easier to defend to a lender

### Debt Service Coverage Check
If financing ~70% of purchase price via SBA 7(a) at 6.5–7.5% over 10 years:
- Annual debt service ≈ ~13–15% of loan amount
- For a $600K loan: ~$78,000–$90,000/yr in debt service
- Business needs SDE of at least 1.25x debt service = $97,500–$112,500 minimum to service debt
- Any remaining SDE above that is the buyer's actual return
- Below 1.0x debt service coverage = lender will reject; 1.0x–1.25x = borderline

## VALUATION ADJUSTMENT WORKSHEET (APPLY TO EACH DEAL)

Start at 2.75x SDE (midpoint of fair range)
Then adjust:

```
Base:                          +2.75x

Written multi-year contracts?   +0.0x to +0.5x
No client concentration risk?   +0.0x to +0.4x
Proven GM in place?             +0.0x to +0.5x
15+ year history?               +0.0x to +0.3x
Revenue growth 3+ years?        +0.0x to +0.4x

Business < 5 yrs old?          -0.3x to -0.5x
High concentration (>30%)?      -0.3x to -0.5x
Owner-dependent ops?            -0.3x to -0.5x
Month-to-month contracts?       -0.2x to -0.4x
Revenue declined?               -0.3x to -0.5x
No seller financing?            -0.1x to -0.2x

Justified multiple:             [Sum]
Justified price:                [Justified multiple × verified SDE]
Asking price:                   [From listing]
Gap:                            [Asking - Justified = your negotiation room]
```

## CAR WASH VALUATION BENCHMARKS

### SDE Multiples by Model

| Model | SDE Multiple Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Self-service/bay | 2.5x–3.5x | Lower labor, simpler ops, but declining model in many markets |
| Express/tunnel | 3.0x–4.5x | Highest throughput, subscription premium, equipment-heavy |
| Full-service | 2.0x–3.5x | Labor-intensive, lower absentee viability |
| Flex-serve (express + detail) | 2.5x–4.0x | Hybrid model, depends on detail revenue % |

### Revenue Multiples

| Model | Revenue Multiple Range |
|---|---|
| Express/tunnel with membership | 0.8x–1.5x |
| Self-service | 0.4x–0.8x |
| Full-service | 0.5x–0.9x |

### Car Wash Premium Factors (+0.2x to +0.5x each)

| Factor | Multiple Lift | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Active membership base > 1,000 members, < 5% monthly churn | +0.3x–0.5x | The key value driver for express washes |
| Real estate included, traffic count > 30K cars/day | +0.2x–0.4x | Location is everything |
| Water reclamation system installed | +0.1x–0.2x | Regulatory compliance + 30–50% water cost savings |
| Equipment age < 5 years on tunnel/wash systems | +0.2x–0.3x | Reduces near-term capex risk |
| Multi-year fleet contracts | +0.2x–0.3x | Recurring B2B revenue |

### Car Wash Discount Factors (-0.2x to -0.5x each)

| Factor | Discount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Equipment age > 10 years (tunnel replacement: $500K–$2M) | -0.3x–0.5x | Major capex imminent |
| No membership/subscription program | -0.2x–0.3x | Missing key revenue stability mechanism |
| Weather-dependent geography without normalization data | -0.1x–0.2x | Can't assess true baseline revenue |
| Lease with < 5 years remaining | -0.2x–0.4x | Location risk |
| Environmental compliance issues | -0.3x–0.5x | Potential remediation costs |
| Single-bay self-service | -0.2x–0.3x | Limited growth potential |

### Car Wash Sanity Checks
- SDE should be 25–40% of revenue for express/tunnel (lower labor intensity)
- SDE should be 15–25% of revenue for full-service (labor-heavy)
- Revenue per express tunnel wash: $8–$18 average ticket
- Membership revenue should be 40–60% of total for a mature express wash
- If membership churn > 8%/month, the membership base is not sticky

## LAUNDROMAT VALUATION BENCHMARKS

### SDE Multiples by Model

| Model | SDE Multiple Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Unattended self-service | 2.5x–4.0x | Best absentee model; lease is critical |
| Attended self-service | 2.0x–3.0x | Labor adds cost, marginal absentee improvement |
| With wash-dry-fold (WDF) service | 2.0x–3.5x | WDF adds revenue but requires staff |
| Multi-location portfolio | 3.0x–4.5x | Operational synergies, management cost amortized |

### Revenue Multiples

| Model | Revenue Multiple Range |
|---|---|
| Self-service (card system, verified revenue) | 0.8x–1.2x |
| Self-service (coin-only, unverifiable) | 0.5x–0.8x (discount for verification risk) |
| With WDF service | 0.6x–1.0x |

### Laundromat Premium Factors (+0.2x to +0.5x each)

| Factor | Multiple Lift | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Card/app payment system with transaction logs | +0.2x–0.4x | Verifiable revenue = buyer confidence |
| Lease > 10 years remaining with renewal option | +0.3x–0.5x | The single biggest premium driver |
| Equipment age < 7 years across all machines | +0.2x–0.3x | Long remaining useful life |
| Revenue per sq ft > $20/year | +0.1x–0.2x | Above-average utilization |
| High-density renter neighborhood (> 50% renters) | +0.2x–0.3x | Strong demographic support |
| Exclusive use clause in lease | +0.1x–0.2x | No competing laundromat in same center |

### Laundromat Discount Factors (-0.2x to -0.5x each)

| Factor | Discount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Coin-only operation | -0.2x–0.3x | Revenue unverifiable without utility cross-check |
| Lease < 5 years remaining, no renewal option | -0.3x–0.5x | Business may be worthless by loan payoff |
| Equipment age > 12 years | -0.3x–0.5x | Approaching end of useful life (10–15 year span) |
| Utility costs > 35% of revenue | -0.2x–0.3x | Margin erosion, possibly deferred maintenance |
| Declining renter % in surrounding area | -0.2x–0.3x | Customer base shrinking |
| No maintenance logs for equipment | -0.1x–0.2x | Unknown condition risk |

### Laundromat Sanity Checks
- SDE should be 30–45% of revenue for unattended self-service
- SDE should be 20–30% of revenue for attended or WDF models
- Utility costs: 20–30% of revenue is healthy; above 35% is a red flag
- Revenue per machine per day: $10–$30 for washers, $5–$15 for dryers
- Turns per machine per day: 4–6 is normal, 8+ should be verified
- Lease cost: 15–25% of revenue is healthy; above 30% is a concern
