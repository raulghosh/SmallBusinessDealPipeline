# Car Wash Industry Reference Guide
## For Absentee Acquisition Evaluation | SE US Focus (FL, AL, TN, NC, SC)
## Target Revenue: $250K-$3M

Sources: International Carwash Association (ICA), Professional Carwashing & Detailing,
Auto Laundry News, IBISWorld 81192, Grand View Research, Southeast Car Wash Association,
NRDC Water Efficiency Standards, state DEQ/DHEC records

## 1. BUSINESS MODELS

### Express/Exterior Tunnel

The dominant model for acquisitions. Customer stays in the car, conveyor pulls the vehicle
through a 120-160 ft tunnel in 3-5 minutes. Volume-driven.

| Metric | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue | $800K-$3M+ |
| Cars per day | 300-800 (top sites: 1,000+) |
| Revenue per car (blended) | $10-$18 |
| Employees | 4-8 per shift (loaders, dryer attendants, site manager) |
| Net margin (stabilized) | 25-40% |
| Capex to build new | $3M-$7M (land + construction + equipment) |
| Acquisition price range | $1.5M-$6M+ (varies heavily by membership base and RE) |
| SDE multiple | 3.5x-5.5x (membership-heavy operations command premiums) |

**Pros for absentee buyer:** Highest absentee viability. Systemized operations. Low labor
relative to revenue. Membership model creates predictable recurring revenue. Site manager
+ 4-6 hourly employees can run the entire operation. Equipment does the work, not people.

**Cons:** High capex. Equipment failures cause immediate revenue loss (tunnel down = no
revenue). Weather-dependent volume. Requires high-traffic location.

### Full-Service

Customer drops off the car. Includes exterior wash, interior vacuum, wipe-down, and
sometimes detailing. Labor-intensive.

| Metric | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue | $500K-$2M |
| Cars per day | 100-300 |
| Revenue per car (blended) | $18-$35 |
| Employees | 12-25 per shift |
| Net margin (stabilized) | 10-20% |
| Labor as % of revenue | 35-45% |
| SDE multiple | 2.0x-3.5x |

**Pros for absentee buyer:** Very few. Higher revenue per car but eaten by labor costs.

**Cons:** Poor absentee viability. Heavy labor management (hiring, training, turnover).
Employee theft is a documented industry problem at full-service washes. High workers' comp
exposure. Turnover rates commonly 100-200% annually. Margin compression from minimum wage
increases. This model is actively declining in the industry -- many are converting to
express or flex-serve.

**Verdict: Avoid for absentee H1B buyer unless converting to express/flex-serve.**

### Self-Service / Bay Wash

Customer drives into an open bay and uses a wand, foam brush, or coin-operated equipment.
Typically 4-8 bays plus 1-2 automatic bays.

| Metric | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue | $150K-$500K (4-6 bay site) |
| Revenue per bay per year | $30K-$70K |
| Employees | 0-1 (part-time attendant or none) |
| Net margin (stabilized) | 30-50% (if RE is owned) |
| Capex per bay (new equipment) | $50K-$150K |
| SDE multiple | 2.5x-4.0x |

**Pros for absentee buyer:** Excellent absentee viability. Near-zero labor. Can be managed
with weekly visits for maintenance and cash collection. Coin/card payment systems reduce
theft exposure. Low complexity. Multiple sites can be managed by one part-time technician.

**Cons:** Revenue ceiling is low -- most self-service sites fall below the $250K target
floor unless multi-site. Declining consumer demand (customers prefer the convenience of
tunnel washes). Vandalism and vagrancy are real issues, especially at unattended sites.
Difficult to add membership/recurring revenue. Limited upside.

**Verdict: Good for absentee if bundled as multi-site portfolio. Poor as single-site
acquisition unless priced very low.**

### Flex-Serve (Hybrid)

Express exterior tunnel plus optional interior cleaning bays staffed by detailers. Customer
can choose exterior-only (express) or add interior services. Growing model.

| Metric | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Annual revenue | $1M-$3M+ |
| Cars per day | 250-600 |
| Revenue per car (blended) | $14-$25 |
| Employees | 6-15 per shift |
| Net margin (stabilized) | 20-30% |
| Labor as % of revenue | 25-35% |
| SDE multiple | 3.0x-5.0x |

**Pros for absentee buyer:** Combines express tunnel absentee viability with higher
revenue per car. Interior services are optional add-ons, so the core tunnel runs with
minimal staff even if you scale back interior. Good upsell path.

**Cons:** More labor than pure express. Interior service quality requires supervision.
Operationally more complex -- two service flows to manage.

**Verdict: Good for absentee if the tunnel generates 60%+ of revenue independently of
interior services.**

## 2. EQUIPMENT & CAPEX

### Tunnel Equipment

| Component | Cost (New) | Lifespan | Replacement Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Complete tunnel system (conveyor, arches, wraps, blowers) | $500K-$1.5M | 10-15 years | Major rebuild at 7-10 years |
| Conveyor chain and rail system | $80K-$200K | 8-12 years | Chain replacement every 5-7 years |
| High-pressure arch systems (6-10 arches) | $15K-$40K each | 7-10 years | Nozzle/pump rebuild every 3-5 years |
| Wrap-around brushes / cloth friction | $8K-$25K each | 3-5 years | Cloth/foam replacement annually |
| Blower/dryer system (final stage) | $30K-$80K | 10-15 years | Motor rebuild every 5-7 years |
| Spot-free rinse / RO system | $20K-$60K | 7-10 years | Membrane replacement every 2-3 years |
| POS / gate / RFID membership system | $30K-$80K | 5-8 years | Software updates ongoing |
| LED signage and menu boards | $10K-$30K | 5-7 years | As needed |

**Total tunnel equipment replacement reserve: budget 5-8% of revenue annually.**

Major equipment manufacturers: PDQ (Dover), Washworld, Sonny's (dominant in SE US),
MacNeil (NCS), Tommy Car Wash Systems. Sonny's is headquartered in Tamarac, FL and has
the largest service network in the SE -- this matters for parts and service response time.

### Self-Service Bay Equipment

| Component | Cost (New) | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|
| In-bay automatic (touchless or soft-touch) | $80K-$150K per bay | 8-12 years |
| Self-service wand/boom system per bay | $15K-$30K per bay | 10-15 years |
| High-pressure pump system | $5K-$15K | 5-8 years |
| Coin/token/card payment system per bay | $3K-$8K | 5-7 years |
| Vacuum stations (per island) | $3K-$8K | 5-8 years |

### Chemical Systems

| Component | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chemical dispensing system (tunnel) | $15K-$40K | Proportional to tunnel length |
| Chemical cost per car (express) | $0.30-$0.80 | Varies by wash package |
| Chemical cost per car (full-service) | $0.50-$1.20 | Interior products add cost |
| Chemical storage tanks and containment | $5K-$15K | Must meet local fire/chemical codes |
| Annual chemical spend (express, 400 cars/day) | $45K-$90K | 3-6% of revenue |

Dominant chemical suppliers in SE US: Blendco, Turtle Wax Pro, Simoniz, National
Chemical Laboratories (NCL). Negotiate volume contracts -- chemical cost per car can
vary 30-40% based on supplier relationship.

### Water Reclamation Systems

| System Type | Cost | Water Savings | Payback |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic settling/filtration (required in most jurisdictions) | $20K-$50K | 50-60% | 2-3 years |
| Advanced reclaim with biological treatment | $60K-$150K | 70-85% | 3-5 years |
| Full closed-loop (zero discharge) | $100K-$250K | 90%+ | 5-8 years |

Water usage without reclaim: 35-50 gallons per car. With reclaim: 10-20 gallons per car.
In FL, SC, and NC, water reclamation is either required or provides significant utility
cost savings. In drought-prone areas, it can be the difference between staying open or
receiving a shutoff order.

### Assessing Equipment Condition During Due Diligence

Request these documents:
1. **Equipment purchase dates and invoices** -- establishes actual age vs. claimed age
2. **Maintenance logs** -- a well-run wash has weekly/monthly maintenance records. No logs
   is a red flag indicating deferred maintenance.
3. **Parts replacement history** -- frequency of breakdowns, cost of parts over last 3 years
4. **Conveyor chain measurement** -- chain stretch beyond manufacturer spec means imminent
   replacement ($80K-$200K)
5. **Water quality reports** -- mineral buildup (especially in FL with hard water) accelerates
   equipment degradation
6. **Chemical system calibration records** -- miscalibrated systems waste chemicals and
   damage equipment

**Rule of thumb:** If equipment is past 60% of its expected lifespan with no major
rebuild history, budget for a full replacement within 3-5 years. Discount the acquisition
price by the estimated replacement cost, prorated.

## 3. REVENUE DRIVERS

### Membership / Unlimited Wash Plans (The Key Value Driver)

This is the single most important factor in car wash valuation. A strong membership base
transforms a weather-dependent, transactional business into a predictable recurring revenue
stream. Buyers pay premiums for membership-heavy operations.

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Members as % of total revenue | <20% | 20-35% | 35-55% | 55%+ |
| Active members (express tunnel) | <500 | 500-1,200 | 1,200-2,500 | 2,500+ |
| Monthly membership price (SE US) | -- | $25-$35 basic | $35-$50 mid | $50-$70 premium |
| Monthly churn rate | >10% | 6-10% | 3-6% | <3% |
| Member wash frequency | 1-2x/month | 2-3x/month | 3-5x/month | 5+/month |
| Revenue per member per month | -- | $25-$35 | $35-$50 | $50+ |
| Customer acquisition cost (CAC) | -- | $15-$30 | -- | -- |
| Lifetime value (LTV) at 4% churn | -- | -- | $625-$1,250 | -- |

**Membership economics example (1,500 members at $40/month average):**
- Monthly recurring revenue: $60,000
- Annual membership revenue: $720,000
- At 4% monthly churn: need to acquire 60 new members/month to maintain base
- At $20 CAC: $1,200/month acquisition cost to maintain (trivial vs. revenue)

**Why this matters for valuation:** A car wash doing $1.2M revenue with 60% from
memberships will sell at 4.5-5.5x SDE. The same revenue with 15% from memberships
might sell at 3.0-3.5x SDE. The membership base IS the asset.

**Due diligence on membership:** Request 24 months of membership data:
- Total active members by month
- New sign-ups per month
- Cancellations per month
- Churn rate trend (improving or deteriorating)
- Revenue per member trend
- Payment failure rate (failed credit cards -- should be <5%)
- Membership tier distribution (are members on cheapest plan or upselling?)

### Single-Wash / Retail Pricing

| Wash Level | Typical Price (SE US) | Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Basic exterior | $8-$12 | 70-80% |
| Mid-tier (tri-color foam, wheel cleaner, etc.) | $13-$18 | 72-82% |
| Premium (ceramic coat, tire shine, underbody) | $18-$28 | 75-85% |
| Ultimate / works package | $25-$35 | 78-88% |

Average revenue per single-wash car: $12-$16 in SE US.
Average revenue per member visit: $0 incremental (they already paid monthly).
This is why member visit frequency matters -- high-frequency members have lower
effective revenue per visit but the monthly payment is guaranteed.

### Detailing & Add-On Services

| Service | Price Range | Margin | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Express interior vacuum + wipe | $15-$30 | 50-60% | Flex-serve upsell |
| Full detail (interior + exterior) | $150-$350 | 40-55% | Labor-intensive |
| Ceramic coating application | $50-$150 | 60-75% | High margin, growing demand |
| Headlight restoration | $30-$60 | 70-80% | Quick upsell |
| Fleet wash contracts | $5-$10/vehicle | 65-75% | Volume play, often 50+ vehicles |

**For absentee buyers:** Express add-ons (ceramic spray in tunnel, tire shine upgrade)
are good -- they are automated and boost revenue per car without labor. Detailing services
requiring manual labor are operationally burdensome and should not be a primary revenue
driver for an absentee operation.

### Fleet Accounts

Fleet contracts (delivery companies, rental car agencies, dealerships, municipal vehicles)
provide steady base volume. Typical fleet pricing is 40-60% below retail single-wash price,
but the volume is consistent and weather-independent.

A healthy fleet book: 5-15 accounts generating 10-20% of total volume. Concentration risk
applies -- no single fleet account should exceed 10% of revenue.

|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | **100%** | |
| Water | 1-3% | With reclaim system; 3-5% without |
| Chemicals | 3-6% | Negotiate supplier contracts aggressively |
| Labor (hourly + site manager) | 15-25% | Express: 15-20%. Flex-serve: 20-25% |
| Site manager salary | Included in labor | $40K-$55K in SE US (see Section 8) |
| Utilities (electric, gas, sewer) | 3-5% | Blowers and water heating are biggest draws |

| Equipment maintenance & repair | 5-8% | Budget higher for older equipment |
| Insurance (GL, property, workers' comp) | 2-4% | Workers' comp varies by state |
| Credit card processing fees | 2.5-3.5% | 70-85% of transactions are card/app |
| Marketing / membership acquisition | 2-4% | Ongoing member acquisition |
| Property taxes (if RE owned) | 1-3% | Varies by county; FL has no state income tax |

| Miscellaneous / admin | 1-2% | Accounting, legal, supplies |
| **Total operating expenses** | **50-75%** | |
| **Net operating income / SDE** | **25-40%** | **Express tunnel target: 30%+** |

### Full-Service P&L Benchmark

| Line Item | % of Revenue | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Labor | 35-45% | The margin killer |
| Water + chemicals | 5-8% | Interior products add cost |
| All other operating costs | 20-30% | Similar to express |
| **Net operating income / SDE** | **10-20%** | **Significantly lower than express** |

### Self-Service P&L Benchmark

| Line Item | % of Revenue | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Labor | 0-5% | Part-time attendant or contract maintenance |
| Water + chemicals | 5-10% | Less efficient per car than tunnel |
| Utilities | 5-8% | Heating water for individual bays |
| Equipment maintenance | 8-12% | Vandalism and wear from public use |
| All other operating costs | 15-25% | |
| **Net operating income / SDE** | **30-50%** | **If RE is owned; 15-30% if leased** |

### Cost Red Flags

- **Labor above 25% for express tunnel:** Overstaffed or paying above market.
- **Chemicals above 8%:** Supplier contract is bad or system is miscalibrated.
- **Water above 5%:** Reclaim system is not working or not installed.
- **Maintenance above 10%:** Equipment is aging out. Expect major capex soon.
- **Credit card fees above 4%:** Renegotiate processor or switch to flat-rate.

## 5. WEATHER & SEASONALITY

### SE US Seasonal Revenue Pattern

| Quarter | Revenue Index | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | 90-110% of average | Pollen season in SE (Feb-Apr) is a major wash driver |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | 110-130% of average | Peak season. Pollen + warm weather + longer days |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | 70-90% of average | Hurricane season. Afternoon thunderstorms reduce traffic |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | 85-100% of average | Recovery after hurricane season. Holiday travel bump |

**Annual swing: 30-50% between best and worst months.** In FL, the swing can be wider
(60%+) due to summer rain patterns.

### SE US Weather Advantages vs. Northern States

- No salt/snow: Northern washes depend on winter salt removal -- this creates opposite
  seasonality. SE washes peak in spring, not winter.
- Pollen is the SE equivalent of road salt: Feb-May pollen season drives consistent volume.
- More wash-compatible days per year: SE averages 200-250 good wash days vs. 150-180 in
  the Midwest/Northeast.
- Year-round membership value: Members use washes year-round; no 3-month winter where
  washes are impractical.

### Hurricane Season Impact (Jun 1 - Nov 30)

- Direct hurricane impact: 1-2 weeks of closure per event (power outage, debris, flooding).
  Typical in FL coastal locations. Less frequent inland and in TN.
- Indirect impact: Afternoon summer thunderstorms in FL reduce daily car counts by 20-40%
  in Jun-Sep. Customers avoid washing cars before predicted rain.
- Insurance: Wind/flood insurance in FL coastal zones can add $15K-$50K/year to operating
  costs. Verify current coverage and claims history.
- Power restoration priority: Car washes are low priority for utility restoration after
  storms. Budget for a commercial generator ($20K-$50K) if in FL or coastal SC/NC.

### Normalizing Revenue for Weather

When evaluating financials, do not take a simple 12-month trailing average. Instead:

1. **Request 36 months of monthly revenue data** (minimum 24 months).
2. **Identify anomalous months:** Any month with revenue <60% of the trailing 12-month
   average likely had a weather event (hurricane, extended rain). Get the explanation.
3. **Normalize by substituting:** Replace anomalous months with the average of the same
   month in other years (e.g., average Sep 2023 and Sep 2025 if Sep 2024 had a hurricane).
4. **Adjust membership separately:** Membership revenue should be steady regardless of
   weather. If membership revenue dropped during a weather event, that signals
   cancellations, which is a churn problem, not a weather problem.
5. **Calculate "good weather day" revenue:** Total retail (non-membership) revenue divided
   by number of days without significant rain. This gives the true demand indicator.

## 6. REAL ESTATE CONSIDERATIONS

### Why Real Estate Matters in Car Wash Deals

Most car wash businesses are inseparable from their real estate. Unlike janitorial
businesses (which can operate from any office), a car wash's value is tied to its
physical location. The equipment is bolted to the site. You cannot move a tunnel wash.

**The real estate decision is often more important than the business decision.**

### Location Requirements by Model

| Factor | Express Tunnel | Self-Service | Full-Service |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traffic count (cars/day past site) | 25,000+ (ideal: 40,000+) | 15,000+ | 20,000+ |
| Lot size | 0.75-1.5 acres | 0.5-1.0 acres | 0.5-1.0 acres |
| Visibility from road | Critical -- must see signage at 500+ ft | Important | Important |
| Ingress/egress | Right-in/right-out minimum; signal-controlled ideal | Less critical | Moderate |
| Stacking capacity (queue space) | 15-25 cars minimum | N/A | 10-15 cars |
| Proximity to retail/commercial | Strong positive (grocery, gas, retail corridors) | Less important | Moderate |
| Residential density within 3 miles | 30,000+ population ideal | 15,000+ | 20,000+ |
| Median household income | $50K+ | $40K+ | $55K+ |

### Buy RE Separately vs. Included

**Option 1: RE included in the deal.**
Common for smaller operations. The business + RE sells as one package. Valuation should
separate the two: business value (SDE multiple) + RE value (appraised or cap rate).
Watch for sellers who inflate the "business value" by including RE at above-market rates.

**Option 2: RE sold separately / held by seller.**
Seller retains ownership of the land/building and leases back to the buyer. This reduces
the buyer's upfront capital but creates lease dependency risk. Verify:
- Lease term: minimum 10 years remaining with options to renew
- Rent escalation: should not exceed 2-3% annually
- Assignment clause: lease must be assignable if you sell the business later
- Triple-net (NNN) vs. gross: know what you are responsible for (taxes, insurance, CAM)

**Option 3: Buyer purchases RE through separate entity.**
Best structure for H1B buyers: buy the RE in an LLC and lease it to the operating business
(also an LLC). Separates real estate investment from operating risk. Consult with both a
CPA and immigration attorney on this structure.

### RE Valuation Benchmarks (SE US)

| Market | Land value per acre (commercial, high-traffic) | Cap rate for NNN car wash |
|---|---|---|
| FL (metro: Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville) | $500K-$2M+ | 5.5-7.0% |
| FL (non-metro) | $150K-$500K | 6.5-8.0% |
| NC (Charlotte, Raleigh, Wilmington) | $400K-$1.5M | 5.5-7.0% |
| TN (Nashville, Memphis, Knoxville) | $300K-$1M | 6.0-7.5% |
| SC (Charleston, Greenville, Columbia) | $250K-$800K | 6.0-7.5% |
| AL (Birmingham, Huntsville, Mobile) | $150K-$500K | 6.5-8.5% |

## 7. REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT

### Water Discharge Permits

Car washes discharge wastewater containing oils, grease, surfactants, and sediment.
Every state in the SE requires some form of wastewater management.

| State | Primary Authority | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| FL | FL DEP (Department of Environmental Protection) | Industrial wastewater permit required. Must connect to municipal sewer with pretreatment OR operate zero-discharge reclaim. FL is the strictest in the SE. |
| NC | NC DEQ (Department of Environmental Quality) | Stormwater permit required. Wash water cannot enter storm drains. Oil/water separator mandatory. |
| SC | SC DHEC (Dept. of Health and Environmental Control) | Wastewater permit required for discharge to sewer. Reclaim systems encouraged with fee reductions in some municipalities. |
| TN | TN DEC (Dept. of Environment and Conservation) | Sewer discharge permit. Less stringent than FL. Pretreatment required if connecting to municipal system. |
| AL | ADEM (Alabama Dept. of Environmental Management) | General NPDES permit for car washes. Oil/water separator required. Less enforcement than FL/NC. |

**Due diligence:** Request copies of all active environmental permits. Verify they are
current and transferable. Check for any Notices of Violation (NOVs) in the last 5 years.
An NOV can indicate ongoing compliance issues that the buyer inherits.

### Chemical Storage

- **OSHA compliance:** SDS (Safety Data Sheets) for all chemicals on-site.
- **Secondary containment:** Chemical storage areas must have containment to prevent spills
  from reaching drains or soil. Required in all SE states.
- **Above-ground storage tanks:** If the site has fuel or bulk chemical tanks, verify UST/AST
  compliance with state regulations. Phase I Environmental Site Assessment is strongly
  recommended for any site with current or historical fuel storage.

### Environmental Due Diligence Checklist

1. Phase I ESA (Environmental Site Assessment): $2,000-$5,000. Recommended for any
   acquisition that includes real estate. Required by most SBA lenders.
2. Verify no underground storage tanks (USTs) on-site unless properly registered.
3. Check state environmental database for site history (prior uses, contamination records).
4. Confirm grease trap / oil-water separator is present, properly sized, and regularly
   maintained (pump-out records).
5. Verify stormwater management: wash water and stormwater must be separated. If the site
   has no separation, this is a compliance risk and potential retrofit cost ($10K-$50K).

### Zoning & Local Regulations

- Car washes typically require C-2 (General Commercial) or C-3 (Highway Commercial) zoning.
- Some municipalities have specific conditional use permits for car washes due to noise,
  traffic, and water concerns. Verify the CUP is transferable.
- Noise ordinances: blower systems can generate 75-90 dB. Some residential-adjacent sites
  have restricted operating hours. Verify before closing.
- Signage regulations vary significantly by municipality. LED electronic signs (common for
  displaying wash prices/memberships) may require a separate variance.

## 8. ABSENTEE VIABILITY BY MODEL

### Absentee Ranking

| Model | Absentee Rating | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Express/exterior tunnel | **A (Best)** | Equipment does the work. Site manager + 4-6 hourly staff runs the operation. Membership revenue is automated. Daily decisions are routine (staffing, minor equipment issues). |
| Self-service/bay | **A- (Excellent)** | Near-zero labor. Requires only periodic maintenance and cash/card collection. Can hire a part-time tech to cover multiple sites. But revenue ceiling is low. |
| Flex-serve | **B+ (Good)** | Tunnel portion is absentee-friendly. Interior service portion requires more oversight for quality control and labor management. Manageable with a strong site manager. |
| Full-service | **D (Poor)** | Labor-dependent. Quality variance is high. Theft and turnover are constant management problems. Requires hands-on oversight. Not suitable for H1B absentee buyer. |

### Site Manager Role & Compensation (SE US)

The site manager is the critical hire for absentee car wash ownership. This person runs
day-to-day operations: opens/closes, manages hourly staff, handles minor equipment issues,
monitors chemical/water systems, resolves customer complaints, tracks daily car counts.

| Role | Salary Range (SE US) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Site manager (express tunnel) | $40,000-$55,000 base | Often + bonus tied to car count or revenue targets |
| Site manager (flex-serve) | $45,000-$60,000 base | Higher due to labor management complexity |
| Assistant manager | $30,000-$40,000 | Backup coverage for manager days off |
| Maintenance technician | $35,000-$50,000 | Can cover 2-3 sites if geographically close |
| Regional manager (multi-site) | $55,000-$75,000 | Needed at 3+ locations |

**Performance bonus structure (common):** $200-$500/month bonus for hitting car count or
revenue targets. $100-$200/month bonus for maintaining customer satisfaction scores.
Bonuses align the manager's incentives with business performance.

### Absentee Infrastructure Requirements

For an express tunnel to operate without owner involvement:

1. **Site manager with clear authority** -- can hire/fire hourly staff, make equipment
   repair decisions under a pre-approved budget ($500-$1,000), handle customer refunds.
2. **Equipment maintenance contract** -- either in-house technician or third-party service
   agreement (Sonny's, local service companies). Response time matters: 2-4 hour response
   for tunnel-down situations.
3. **POS and management software** -- DRB Systems, Rinsed, Washify, EverWash. These
   provide remote dashboards showing real-time car counts, revenue, membership sign-ups,
   and equipment status. The absentee owner monitors via phone/laptop.
4. **Camera system** -- 8-16 camera system covering tunnel entry/exit, pay stations,
   vacuum area, and chemical room. Cloud-based (accessible remotely). $3K-$10K installed.
5. **Accounting/bookkeeping** -- outsourced monthly. $500-$1,500/month for a single-site
   car wash. Owner reviews P&L monthly, not daily.
6. **Standard operating procedures (SOPs)** -- written procedures for opening, closing,
   equipment checks, chemical mixing, customer escalation. If the seller has no SOPs,
   budget 30-60 days post-close to create them.

### H1B-Specific Considerations

Same principles as the janitorial absentee framework:
- Owner cannot work in the business (no scheduling staff, no running equipment, no
  handling customer complaints directly).
- Board/investor-level oversight only: review financials, approve major expenditures,
  hire/fire the site manager.
- The site manager must be genuinely autonomous -- not a figurehead who calls the owner
  for every decision.
- Structure ownership through an LLC. Consult immigration attorney before closing.

## 9. KEY METRICS TO REQUEST FROM SELLER

### Mandatory Data Requests

Request all of these before making an offer. If the seller cannot provide them, that
itself is a data point (poor record-keeping = higher risk).

**Financial:**
- 3 years of tax returns (business)
- 3 years of monthly P&L statements
- 12-24 months of daily revenue reports (from POS system)
- Breakdown of revenue by source: memberships, retail wash, detailing, fleet, vending
- Credit card processing statements (12 months) -- cross-reference with reported revenue
- Utility bills (12 months) -- cross-reference with reported water/electric costs

**Operational:**
- Cars per day by month (36 months if available, minimum 24)
- Revenue per car by month (total revenue / total cars)
- Membership data: active members by month, sign-ups, cancellations, churn rate, tier mix
- Employee roster: roles, tenure, wages, hours
- Equipment list: make, model, age, purchase date, purchase price
- Equipment maintenance logs (the single most revealing document)
- Chemical purchase invoices (12 months)
- Water usage records (12 months)
- Insurance policies and claims history (5 years)

**Real estate:**
- Lease agreement (if applicable) with all amendments and options
- Property tax bills (3 years)
- Property appraisal (if seller claims RE value)
- Phase I ESA (if available; order one if not)
- Traffic count study (request from state DOT or local planning office)
- Survey and site plan

**Regulatory:**
- All environmental permits (wastewater, stormwater, air if applicable)
- Compliance inspection reports (3 years)

## 10. INDUSTRY TRENDS & COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Consolidation

The car wash industry is in a major consolidation wave. Private equity has been acquiring
car wash chains aggressively since 2018. Major players in the SE:

- **Mister Car Wash** -- 450+ locations nationally, heavy SE presence
- **Zips Car Wash** -- 280+ locations, SE-focused (HQ in Plano, TX)
- **Tidal Wave Auto Spa** -- 200+ locations, HQ in Thomaston, GA (SE dominant)
- **Whistle Express** -- 100+ locations, expanding in SE

**What this means for a buyer:** Competition from well-capitalized chains is increasing.
Independent operators need strong locations and membership bases to compete. A site on a
secondary road with no membership program will struggle against a Tidal Wave or Zips
opening on the main corridor.

**Upside of consolidation:** Independent car washes with strong membership bases and good
real estate are attractive acquisition targets for PE-backed chains. A buyer who improves
operations and grows membership may have a lucrative exit to a chain in 3-5 years at a
premium multiple.

### Technology Trends

- License plate recognition (LPR) for membership identification -- eliminates RFID tags
- Mobile app payments and membership management
- Chemical usage optimization through IoT sensors
- Predictive maintenance systems for tunnel equipment
- Automated labor scheduling based on weather forecasts and traffic patterns

### Membership Model Dominance

The industry is rapidly shifting from transactional (pay-per-wash) to subscription
(unlimited monthly plans). Express tunnels with strong membership programs trade at
meaningfully higher multiples. Any acquisition target without a membership program
represents either a risk (they are behind the industry) or an opportunity (implement
membership to grow revenue 30-50% within 12-18 months).

## APPENDIX: DEAL EVALUATION QUICK REFERENCE

### Immediate Disqualifiers for H1B Absentee Buyer

- Full-service model with no conversion plan to express/flex-serve
- No site manager in place and no clear candidate to hire
- Seller IS the business (all customer relationships, all operational knowledge)
- Environmental violations outstanding or unresolved
- Lease with <5 years remaining and no renewal options
- Equipment past useful life with no capex reserve or price adjustment
- Revenue declining >10% year-over-year with no weather explanation
- Single fleet account >25% of revenue

### Green Lights

- Express tunnel with 35%+ membership revenue and <5% monthly churn
- Site manager with 2+ years tenure willing to stay post-acquisition
- Equipment <5 years old from a major manufacturer (Sonny's, PDQ, Tommy)
- Traffic count >30,000 cars/day with good visibility and ingress/egress
- Owned real estate or long-term lease (10+ years remaining)
- SDE margin >28%
- Seller willing to provide 24+ months of POS data for verification
- Located in growing SE metro area (Nashville, Charlotte, Raleigh, Tampa, etc.)
