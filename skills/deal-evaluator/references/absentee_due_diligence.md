# Absentee Viability Framework — Janitorial Businesses
## Special Focus: H1B Visa Buyers

---

## WHY ABSENTEE VIABILITY IS DIFFERENT FOR H1B BUYERS

An H1B visa holder may own a business as a passive investor but cannot be employed by that
business without a separate work authorization. This means the buyer must ensure that:
1. No operational role is required of them post-acquisition
2. A salaried GM (or equivalent) genuinely runs day-to-day operations
3. Client relationships are institutionalized, not personal
4. The buyer's involvement is limited to ownership oversight (board/investor level)

If the business requires any of the following from the owner, it is NOT suitable for an
H1B buyer, regardless of how attractive the financials look:
- Scheduling crews
- Managing client relationships directly
- Handling payroll or HR disputes
- Conducting quality inspections
- Bidding on new contracts
- Operating any equipment

**Legal note:** Before making any offer, the buyer should consult both a business attorney
and an immigration attorney familiar with H1B passive investment structures.

## ABSENTEE VIABILITY SCORING RUBRIC (0–10)

Score each factor and sum for a composite score.

### Management Infrastructure (0–4 points — most critical)
| Evidence | Points |
|---|---|
| Confirmed salaried GM with documented authority over operations | 4 |
| Operations manager or lead supervisor in place (not GM-level, but close) | 3 |
| Strong crew leads who self-manage, no formal management title | 2 |
| Owner runs operations but claims it takes "only a few hours" | 1 |
| Owner is sole point of contact for everything | 0 |

### Client Relationship Structure (0–3 points)
| Evidence | Points |
|---|---|
| Contracts held in business name, no personal relationship required | 3 |
| Mix: some contractual, some relationship-based with long tenure | 2 |
| Clients are known to the owner personally; relationships unclear | 1 |
| Owner is primary or sole client contact | 0 |

### Business Model (0–2 points)
| Evidence | Points |
|---|---|
| Commercial janitorial with multi-year recurring contracts | 2 |
| Commercial recurring but month-to-month contracts | 1 |
| Primarily residential or project-based work | 0 |

### Transition Plan (0–1 point)
| Evidence | Points |
|---|---|
| Seller offers 3–6 month structured transition including client introductions | 1 |
| Short transition (2–4 weeks) or transition details not discussed | 0 |

### Score Interpretation
| Score | Verdict |
|---|---|
| 8–10 | High absentee viability — suitable for H1B buyer with proper legal structure |
| 5–7 | Medium — workable if GM is hired/confirmed pre-close; requires conditions in LOI |
| 3–4 | Low — requires significant structural changes before suitable for H1B buyer |
| 0–2 | Not suitable — owner-dependent, pass unless completely restructured |

## DUE DILIGENCE QUESTIONS FOR ABSENTEE VIABILITY

Ask these before making any offer:

**Management Structure:**
1. "Does the business currently have a GM or operations manager on payroll? If yes, what is
   their compensation and how long have they been with the company?"
2. "How many hours per week does the current owner spend working *in* the business vs.
   *on* the business? What specifically do those hours consist of?"
3. "Who handles crew scheduling and daily dispatch? Is this documented in software or done
   manually by the owner?"
4. "If the owner was on a 30-day vacation starting tomorrow, what would break? Who would
   handle it?"

**Client Relationships:**
5. "Are client contracts signed in the company name or the owner's personal name?"
6. "For your top 5 clients, how did you acquire them? Are any of these relationships personal
   or social (family, church, club, etc.)?"
7. "When a client has a complaint or service issue, who do they call? Does that number go to
   the owner personally or to an office line?"

**Operational Systems:**
8. "What software does the business use for scheduling, invoicing, and communication?"
9. "Is there a documented operations manual or standard operating procedures for crew leads?"
10. "How are new hires recruited, trained, and onboarded? Who manages this process?"

**Post-Close Risk:**
11. "Would you be willing to include an earnout provision tied to client retention for 12
    months post-close?" (A seller confident in absentee viability will say yes)
12. "Are any key employees aware the business is for sale? What is their likely response?"

## BUSINESS MODELS RANKED BY ABSENTEE SUITABILITY

### Best (High Absentee Suitability)
- **Commercial janitorial with long-term contracts (3+ yr, multi-site)** — Client stickiness
  is built into contract structure, not personal relationships
- **Building services contracts (offices, malls, campuses)** — Scheduled, predictable, low
  client interaction required after onboarding
- **Government/municipal contracts** — Highly institutionalized, least relationship-dependent
- **Healthcare/medical facility cleaning** — Highly regulated, contract-based, rarely personal

### Medium (Requires Conditions)
- **Commercial janitorial, month-to-month** — Recurring but fragile; needs strong GM to
  maintain relationship without owner
- **Multi-service (cleaning + maintenance)** — More touchpoints, but also more revenue
  stability; depends heavily on management depth
- **Post-construction cleaning** — Project-based creates gaps; not pure recurring but often
  driven by B2B GC relationships that can transfer

### Worst (Not Suitable Without Restructuring)
- **Residential cleaning** — Client relationships are almost entirely personal; high churn;
  owner typically manages complaint resolution directly
- **Restaurant/kitchen exhaust cleaning** — Highly relationship-driven, often priced per-job
  with personal negotiation; technically specialized requiring owner oversight
- **Specialty one-off projects** — Not recurring; requires owner to bid and manage each engagement

## STRUCTURING THE DEAL FOR H1B COMPLIANCE

If the business is suitable and the buyer proceeds:

**Legal Entity Structure:** The buyer should own through an LLC or C-Corp where they are
a member/shareholder, not an officer-employee. The GM should be the operator of record.

**Operating Agreement:** Should define the buyer's role as passive investor/owner with no
operational duties. Document that management authority is vested in the GM.

**Employment Agreements:** The GM's contract should include non-compete, non-solicitation,
and key-man provisions to protect the buyer's investment.

**Transition Period:** Request that the seller remain as a paid consultant (not employee)
for 90–180 days, with compensation tied to client retention metrics.

**Immigration Attorney Review:** Have the immigration attorney review the operating
agreement and any management contract before signing. Some SBA lenders require borrower
involvement that could conflict with H1B status — review the loan documents carefully.

## CAR WASH ABSENTEE SUITABILITY

### By Model (ranked best to worst for absentee ownership)

**1. Express/Tunnel with Membership (Absentee Score: 7–9/10)**
The best absentee car wash model. Automated tunnel handles the wash; minimal labor beyond
a site manager and 2–4 line attendants. Subscription revenue is predictable and recurring.
- Requires: Site manager ($40K–$55K in SE US), maintenance tech or service contract, POS
  system with remote monitoring
- H1B compatible: Yes, with proper structure. Site manager handles all operations.
- Key risk: Equipment breakdowns require fast response — need a reliable maintenance plan.

**2. Self-Service Bay (Absentee Score: 7–8/10)**
Very low labor. Revenue from coin/card machines. Main operational need is equipment
maintenance and bay cleaning.
- Requires: Part-time attendant for cleaning/restocking ($12–$16/hr), maintenance person on call
- H1B compatible: Yes. Minimal management needed.
- Key risk: Vandalism and machine breakdown response times.

**3. Flex-Serve / Express + Detail (Absentee Score: 5–7/10)**
Express tunnel is absentee-friendly, but the detail/add-on service line requires labor
management and scheduling.
- Requires: Site manager + detailing staff. More complex scheduling.
- H1B compatible: Marginal. Depends on management depth.
- Key risk: Detail service quality is hard to maintain without hands-on oversight.

**4. Full-Service (Absentee Score: 2–4/10)**
Labor-intensive. Interior cleaning, vacuuming, hand-drying all require staffed teams.
High turnover, quality control challenges.
- NOT recommended for H1B buyers. Too operationally involved.

### Car Wash Absentee Questions
- Is there a site manager currently on staff, and what is their tenure and salary?
- What is the equipment maintenance arrangement (in-house tech, service contract, or ad hoc)?
- Can the POS/wash management system be monitored remotely?
- What happens when the tunnel goes down during peak hours? Who makes the call?
- How many employees handle the line, and what is their average tenure?

## LAUNDROMAT ABSENTEE SUITABILITY

### By Model (ranked best to worst for absentee ownership)

**1. Unattended Self-Service with Card System (Absentee Score: 8–10/10)**
The gold standard for absentee ownership. No staff needed during operating hours. Card/app
systems enable remote monitoring, revenue verification, and machine status alerts.
- Requires: Card/app system for remote monitoring and revenue tracking, maintenance person
  on call (2–4 hours/week typical), security cameras, change machine or card dispenser
- H1B compatible: Excellent. Truly passive ownership.
- Key risk: Equipment breakdowns during operating hours. Need a maintenance person who can
  respond within a few hours. Service contracts with equipment vendors (Speed Queen, Dexter)
  typically include same-day or next-day response.

**2. Attended Self-Service (Absentee Score: 6–7/10)**
Attendant handles customer questions, security, light cleaning. Low skill requirement.
- Requires: Part-time attendant ($12–$16/hr in SE US), maintenance person
- H1B compatible: Yes, with attendant handling day-to-day.
- Key risk: Attendant turnover and finding reliable part-time help.

**3. With Wash-Dry-Fold Service (Absentee Score: 4–6/10)**
WDF adds a labor-intensive service line. Need reliable staff who handle customer garments
with care and consistent turnaround times.
- Requires: WDF attendants + a shift lead/manager. More like a retail operation.
- H1B compatible: Possible but requires strong local management.
- Key risk: WDF quality inconsistency damages reputation quickly.

**4. Pickup & Delivery Model (Absentee Score: 3–5/10)**
Adds logistics complexity: drivers, scheduling, customer communication, vehicle maintenance.
- NOT recommended for first-time absentee buyer.

### Laundromat Absentee Questions
- Is there a card/app payment system with remote access? (If coin-only, you're operating blind)
- Who handles equipment breakdowns currently? Is there a service contract with the manufacturer?
- What is the typical response time for a broken machine?
- Is the current owner on-site regularly? If so, what exactly do they do?
- How is security handled (cameras, hours of operation, neighborhood safety)?
- For WDF operations: who manages the WDF staff, and what is the turnover rate?
