# Insurance Benchmarks — Market Intel Reference

Insurance cost benchmarks for janitorial/commercial cleaning, car wash, and laundromat
verticals in the Southeast US (GA, FL, AL, TN, NC, SC).

Use these benchmarks to:
1. Validate that a deal's stated insurance costs are within a realistic range
2. Estimate realistic insurance costs when they are not stated in the deal financials
3. Flag suspiciously low stated insurance as a potential SDE inflation signal

Data reflects 2025-2026 market conditions for businesses in the $250K-$3M revenue range.
Actual premiums vary by carrier, claims history, employee count, equipment age, and
specific state. Treat these as reasonable proxies for underwriting purposes — not
as guarantees.

## VERTICAL 1: JANITORIAL / COMMERCIAL CLEANING

### Coverage Breakdown

**General Liability (GL)**
- **Benchmark range:** $1,500-$4,000/yr base premium for a small operation
- **Per-employee add-on:** Approximately $400-$700 per full-time equivalent employee
  (janitorial GL is employee-count-sensitive because each employee is a risk exposure at
  client sites)
- **Example:** 10 employees → estimated GL of $5,500-$11,000/yr
- **Notes:**
  - Higher if the business does window cleaning, high-rise work, or elevated surface cleaning
    (adds significant premium — can double or triple base GL)
  - Subcontractor liability endorsement recommended if business uses 1099 workers;
    some policies exclude subcontractor work without endorsement

**Workers Compensation**
- **Rate range:** 1.5%-3% of payroll (national average); state-specific rates apply
  - Georgia: 5-7% for class 9014 (building cleaning)
  - Florida: 6-9% for class 9014 — ELEVATED; factor into any FL deal underwriting
  - Alabama: 4-6% for class 9014 — below average; favorable
  - Tennessee: 4-6% for class 9014 — below average; favorable
  - North Carolina: 5-7% for class 9014 — moderate
  - South Carolina: 4-6% for class 9014 — below average; favorable
- **Examples:**
  - $300K payroll in GA → $15,000-$21,000/yr workers comp
  - $300K payroll in FL → $18,000-$27,000/yr workers comp
- **Note:** Workers comp is the largest insurance cost for janitorial businesses.
  A deal with 10+ employees must have a verifiable workers comp policy. If workers comp
  is absent from stated insurance costs, either it's being underreported or the business
  is misclassifying workers as 1099 (serious legal and operational risk).

**Janitorial Bond (Fidelity / Dishonesty Bond)**
- **Purpose:** Protects clients against employee theft — most commercial contracts require it
- **Range:** $300-$600/yr per $10,000 of bond coverage
- **Typical coverage:** $100,000-$250,000 aggregate for a small-to-mid janitorial firm
- **Annual cost estimate:** $3,000-$15,000/yr depending on bond amount and number of employees
- **Note:** A janitorial business without a bond is essentially unlicensable for commercial
  contracts. If the deal lists $0 for bonding, verify immediately.

**Commercial Auto**
- **Coverage:** Vehicles used for transportation of employees and equipment to client sites
- **Range:** $2,000-$5,000/yr per vehicle (commercial auto, not personal)
- **Notes:**
  - If employees use their own vehicles and are reimbursed per mile, the business may have
    non-owned/hired auto liability coverage instead ($500-$1,500/yr)
  - Businesses that own 5+ vehicles should have a commercial fleet policy

**Umbrella / Excess Liability**
- **Range:** $800-$2,000/yr for a $1M umbrella policy
- **Recommended:** For any business with $500K+ revenue

**Total Insurance as % of Revenue — Janitorial**
- **Well-run operation (no claims history, standard cleaning only):** 2%-4% of revenue
- **Higher-risk operations (window cleaning, elevated work, high headcount):** 4%-7% of revenue
- **Uninsured / underinsured (red flag territory):** Below 1.5% of revenue (for operations
  with any employees) is almost certainly indicating inadequate coverage

### Janitorial — Key Coverage Risks
1. **Slip and fall at client sites** — most common GL claim; a customer or client employee
   falls on a freshly mopped surface; claim sizes range from $10,000 to $1M+
2. **Employee theft** — bonding covers this; unbonded businesses create liability and
   contract termination risk
3. **Property damage at client sites** — broken equipment, stained carpets, damaged furniture
4. **Subcontractor liability** — if 1099 workers cause damage; coverage gap if policy
   excludes subcontractor work
5. **EPLI (Employment Practices Liability)** — wage theft claims, discrimination claims,
   especially relevant for businesses with large hourly workforces
6. **Cyber liability** — relevant if the business handles client access credentials or
   security codes for client facilities

### Janitorial — Stated Insurance Below Benchmarks: Flag Criteria
Flag for investigation if stated annual insurance is below:
- $3,000/yr for any employee-based business (below this, coverage is almost certainly
  inadequate or 1099 misclassification is hiding workers comp exposure)
- 1.5% of revenue for businesses with 5+ employees
- Workers comp appears absent or is below expected range for the stated employee count
  and state

## VERTICAL 2: CAR WASH

### Coverage Breakdown

**General Liability (GL)**
- **Benchmark range:** $3,000-$8,000/yr
- **Key driver:** Volume of cars processed; higher-volume tunnels pay more
- **Express tunnel (high volume):** $5,000-$8,000/yr baseline
- **Self-service / in-bay:** $3,000-$5,000/yr baseline
- **Notes:**
  - Vehicle damage claims (scratched paint, broken mirrors, bent antennas) are the most
    common GL claims for car wash operations
  - Some carriers offer vehicle damage coverage as a separate product; operators must
    decide whether to self-insure minor vehicle damage claims or carry specific coverage

**Property Insurance**
- **Benchmark range:** $5,000-$15,000/yr (varies heavily by structure value and equipment value)
- **Key drivers:**
  - Conveyor/tunnel system value (express tunnel equipment: $500K-$2M replacement value)
  - Building/structure (if not tenant-operated)
  - Equipment and FF&E
- **Florida note:** Property coverage for car washes in FL is significantly elevated (30-60%
  higher than inland Southeast) due to hurricane/wind exposure. In some Florida markets,
  property coverage is difficult to place without surplus lines carriers.
- **Hurricane endorsements:** In coastal FL and coastal SC, wind/hurricane coverage may
  be a separate policy or endorsement — budget $2,000-$8,000/yr extra for coastal locations

**Workers Compensation**
- **Rate range:** 2%-4% of payroll (car wash is lower hazard than janitorial)
- **Class code:** Typically classified as "Automobile Washing and Polishing" (~class 3808)
- **Example:** 6 employees at $35,000/yr each = $210,000 payroll → $4,200-$8,400/yr

**Environmental Liability**
- **Benchmark range:** $2,000-$6,000/yr
- **Coverage:** Covers chemical storage spills, wastewater treatment issues, stormwater
  violations, and third-party bodily injury/property damage from environmental incidents
- **Notes:**
  - Mandatory for car wash operations with chemical storage tanks over certain thresholds
  - Spill containment incidents at car wash sites can trigger expensive DEP/EPD cleanup
    orders without this coverage
  - FL operators: higher premiums due to stricter DEP oversight
  - Some carriers bundle environmental with GL; verify coverage exists even if bundled

**Equipment Breakdown (Mechanical Breakdown Insurance)**
- **Benchmark range:** $1,500-$4,000/yr
- **Coverage:** Covers sudden and accidental mechanical breakdown of conveyor, motor, and
  electronic control systems
- **Critical for express tunnels:** A conveyor failure during peak hours can cost $5,000-
  $50,000 in lost revenue and emergency repair costs. This coverage has an excellent
  ROI for high-volume operations.

**Commercial Auto**
- Minimal for most car washes (no customer vehicles transported)
- Relevant if the business operates mobile detailing as an add-on service

**Umbrella**
- **Range:** $1,000-$3,000/yr for a $1M-$2M umbrella
- Strongly recommended given vehicle damage claim exposure

**Total Insurance as % of Revenue — Car Wash**
- **Well-run operation:** 3%-6% of revenue
- **Florida operation or high-equipment-value tunnel:** 4%-8% of revenue
- **Red flag territory:** Below 2% of revenue for an express tunnel with 6+ employees and
  heavy equipment is almost certainly indicating coverage gaps

### Car Wash — Key Coverage Risks
1. **Vehicle damage claims** — scratched paint, broken mirrors, wheel cover damage;
   most common claim; some operators self-insure under $500 per claim
2. **Equipment breakdown / conveyor failure** — sudden mechanical failure; significant
   lost revenue during downtime
3. **Environmental spill** — chemical tank leak, wastewater discharge violation; can
   trigger DEP/EPD enforcement action and remediation costs
4. **Slip and fall** — customer or employee slips on wet surfaces
5. **Hurricane / wind damage** (FL and coastal SC) — the largest single risk for FL
   car wash operators; conveyor systems and roofing are highly exposed
6. **Fire** — chemical storage areas carry fire risk; particularly relevant for full-service
   operations with large chemical inventories

### Car Wash — Stated Insurance Below Benchmarks: Flag Criteria
Flag for investigation if stated annual insurance is below:
- $5,000/yr for an express tunnel operation
- $3,000/yr for a self-service or in-bay operation
- Environmental liability appears absent (any car wash with chemical storage needs this)
- Property coverage appears absent or is clearly below replacement value
- Florida-based car wash without mention of hurricane/wind endorsement

## VERTICAL 3: LAUNDROMAT

### Coverage Breakdown

**General Liability (GL)**
- **Benchmark range:** $1,500-$3,500/yr
- **Key driver:** Square footage and patron volume
- **Notes:**
  - Slip and fall on wet floors is the primary GL exposure
  - Laundromats are lower-risk than janitorial or car wash from a GL perspective —
    they're not sending employees to client sites or processing moving vehicles

**Property Insurance**
- **Benchmark range:** $3,000-$8,000/yr
- **Key drivers:**
  - Total equipment value (20-40 machines at $2,000-$8,000 each = $40,000-$320,000 replacement)
  - Building contents vs. real estate (if tenant, contents-only policy)
  - Location (weather exposure; coastal SC/FL adds premium)
- **Included equipment examples:** Washers, dryers, payment systems (coin/card), water
  heating systems, HVAC, signage
- **Notes:**
  - Machine flooding (washer supply line failure) is a common property claim
  - Dryer lint buildup is a documented fire risk — insurers may require documentation
    of regular lint trap maintenance

**Workers Compensation**
- **Rate range:** 1.5%-2.5% of payroll — among the lowest of any service vertical
- **Minimal for unattended operations:** An unattended laundromat with no employees has
  no workers comp exposure (owner is typically exempt as sole proprietor or LLC member)
- **For attended operations (part-time attendant):** $10,000-$20,000 payroll → $150-$500/yr

**Umbrella**
- **Benchmark range:** $800-$1,500/yr for a $1M umbrella
- Strongly recommended — the incremental cost is minimal and the coverage protection
  is meaningful for a business with high foot traffic

**Total Insurance as % of Revenue — Laundromat**
- **Well-run unattended operation:** 2%-4% of revenue
- **Attended or WDF operation:** 2.5%-5% of revenue
- **Red flag territory:** Below 1.5% of revenue for any attended laundromat; below 1%
  for any laundromat with significant machine value is likely indicating underinsurance

### Laundromat — Key Coverage Risks
1. **Slip and fall** — wet floors are constant; this is the #1 liability exposure
2. **Machine flooding / water damage** — washer supply line or drain failures can damage
   floors, walls, neighboring units (if in a strip mall), and customer garments
3. **Fire** — dryer lint buildup is a documented fire cause; insurers may require
   annual certification of lint trap maintenance
4. **Theft / vandalism** — coin collection theft and machine vandalism are ongoing risks;
   card payment systems reduce coin theft but electronic payment hardware introduces
   new vandalism target
5. **Garment damage** — customer claims for damaged clothing; GL typically covers this
6. **Business interruption** — if major machine failures or property damage cause
   extended closure, business interruption coverage can cover lost revenue

### Laundromat — Stated Insurance Below Benchmarks: Flag Criteria
Flag for investigation if stated annual insurance is below:
- $2,000/yr for any attended laundromat
- $1,500/yr for an unattended laundromat with >20 machines
- Property coverage appears below the replacement value of the machine inventory
- No umbrella coverage for a high-traffic location

## How to Flag Below-Benchmark Insurance in Deal Analysis

### Step 1: Extract stated insurance from deal financials
Look in the deal evaluator memo for stated annual insurance costs. Common sources:
- P&L line items: "insurance," "liability insurance," "workers compensation," "bond"
- Owner add-backs that include insurance adjustments
- Broker disclosures in deal materials

### Step 2: Calculate stated insurance as % of revenue
Divide total stated annual insurance by total revenue. Compare to benchmark range:

| Vertical | Red Flag Below | Benchmark Range | High End |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| Janitorial | <1.5% revenue | 2%-4% revenue | 4%-7% |
| Car Wash | <2% revenue | 3%-6% revenue | 4%-8% (FL) |
| Laundromat | <1.5% revenue | 2%-4% revenue | 2.5%-5% (WDF) |

### Step 3: Flag if below benchmark
If stated insurance is below the low end of the benchmark range, include the following
in the Market Intel Report:

> **INSURANCE FLAG:** The deal's stated annual insurance of $[X] represents [Y]% of
> revenue, which is below the [Z-Z]% benchmark range for [vertical] businesses of this
> size in [state]. This raises one of three concerns: (1) coverage is inadequate and
> the buyer will face a true-up at renewal; (2) certain coverages (e.g., workers comp,
> bonding) are missing and the SDE is overstated; or (3) the business has unusually
> favorable claims history that has earned premium discounts. Recommend obtaining
> current insurance declarations pages and certificate of insurance during diligence.

### Step 4: Do not be alarmist for borderline cases
If stated insurance is within 10-15% of the low benchmark, note it as "insurance costs
at the low end of benchmarks — verify current policies and renewals during diligence"
rather than flagging it as a red flag. Reserve hard flags for cases clearly below the
benchmarks.

## State-Specific Insurance Adjustments

| State | Workers Comp Modifier | Property Risk | Environmental | Overall Premium Index |
|-------|----------------------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| GA | 1.0x (baseline) | LOW | LOW | 1.0x |
| FL | 1.3-1.6x | HIGH (coastal) | MEDIUM-HIGH | 1.4x average |
| AL | 0.8-0.9x | LOW | LOW | 0.85x |
| TN | 0.9-1.0x | LOW | LOW-MEDIUM | 0.9x |
| NC | 1.0-1.1x | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | 1.05x |
| SC | 0.85-0.95x | MEDIUM (coastal) | LOW | 0.9x (inland), 1.2x (coastal) |

**Using the modifier:** Apply the state modifier to the baseline benchmark range to
estimate state-adjusted insurance costs.
- Example: Janitorial in FL, $600K revenue, 8 employees
  - Baseline benchmark: 3% of revenue = $18,000/yr
  - FL modifier: 1.4x
  - Adjusted estimate: $18,000 × 1.4 = $25,200/yr
  - If deal states $8,000/yr insurance → MAJOR RED FLAG

## Insurance Red Flags Summary

| Flag | Severity | What It Could Mean |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Insurance cost <1.5% of revenue (janitorial with employees) | HIGH | Workers comp likely missing; misclassification risk |
| No workers comp expense listed (janitorial 5+ employees) | HIGH | 1099 misclassification; uninsured liability exposure |
| No bond listed (janitorial with commercial clients) | HIGH | Business cannot legally hold commercial contracts |
| No environmental liability (car wash) | MEDIUM-HIGH | Regulatory exposure; one incident = costly remediation |
| No property coverage or clearly below replacement value | MEDIUM | Machine/equipment replacement not covered |
| FL car wash without hurricane/wind coverage | HIGH | Single storm event could be catastrophic uninsured loss |
| Insurance cost rising rapidly year-over-year (>20%/yr) | MEDIUM | Poor claims history; future insurability risk |
| Insurance placed through non-standard surplus lines market | MEDIUM | Carrier instability; coverage may not renew |
