# SBA 7(a) Eligibility Reference — H1B Buyer Edition

## SBA 7(a) Loan Basics

**Maximum loan amount:** $5,000,000 (more than sufficient for target deals in the $500K-$4M range)

**Loan terms by asset type:**
- Working capital / equipment / business acquisition: 10 years
- Real estate included in purchase: up to 25 years (extends term, reduces monthly payment significantly)

**Interest rates (2025-2026 estimates):**
- Variable rate: Prime + 2.75% = approximately 10.5-11% in current environment
- Fixed rates available but typically higher
- Rates quoted at application — always get a current lender quote

**SBA guarantee structure:**
- SBA guarantees 75-85% of the loan amount to the lender
- This allows lenders to approve deals they otherwise couldn't
- The guarantee fee is paid by the borrower (rolled into loan)

**Minimum equity injection for acquisitions:**
- Standard acquisition: 10% minimum
- Certain industries or higher-risk deals: 20-30% required
- Janitorial, car wash, laundromat: typically 10-15% acceptable with strong DSCR
- Buyers with limited operating experience in the vertical: lender may require 20%

**DSCR minimum:** 1.25x
- The business must generate $1.25 in adjusted NOI for every $1.00 of annual debt service
- For H1B buyers: DSCR is calculated AFTER subtracting GM salary (see `dscr_calculator.md`)
- Lenders often want 1.25x-1.35x as a buffer; below 1.25x is a near-certain decline

**Personal guarantee:**
- Required for all owners with 20% or more equity stake
- H1B holders giving a personal guarantee is standard and is NOT a complication
- The guarantee means the buyer's personal assets back the loan if the business defaults

## H1B Visa Holder Eligibility — CRITICAL SECTION

### The Fundamental Rule
H1B visa holders CAN own US businesses. There is no law or SBA regulation that prohibits
business ownership based on visa status. Ownership is a property right, not employment.

H1B holders CAN apply for and receive SBA 7(a) loans as **passive investors**.

### The Active Management Requirement
The SBA requires that a business receiving a 7(a) loan be actively managed by a US citizen
or lawful permanent resident (green card holder). This is the critical constraint for H1B buyers.

**What "active management" means:**
- Day-to-day operational authority
- Hiring and firing decisions
- Client/contract management
- Financial oversight and decisions
- The person whose departure would materially disrupt operations

**What "active management" does NOT mean:**
- A nominal title with no real authority (SBA will look past paper titles)
- A figurehead GM who is secretly supervised by the H1B owner
- Board-level oversight only (this is fine for the H1B owner)

**The practical implication:** The H1B buyer must hire a genuine, compensated, market-rate
GM or operator with real authority before the loan can close. This person must be a US citizen
or permanent resident.

### What the H1B Buyer CAN Do
- Own equity (any percentage — no restriction)
- Serve on the board or as a director
- Make strategic decisions (not day-to-day operations)
- Review financial reports
- Approve major capital expenditures
- Receive profit distributions
- Give a personal guarantee on the loan
- Be a signatory on business bank accounts

### What the H1B Buyer CANNOT Do
- Serve as the active GM / daily operator
- Be employed by the business in any capacity (H1B is employer-specific)
- Be the "key person" the lender relies on for operational continuity
- Manage employees directly in an ongoing capacity

### The Personal Guarantee Nuance
Some immigration attorneys have raised questions about whether signing a personal guarantee
constitutes "employment" under H1B regulations. The consensus view is that a personal guarantee
is an investor obligation, not employment — but the buyer should get written confirmation from
their immigration attorney that the transaction structure is H1B-compliant before proceeding.

### Immigration Attorney Review — Non-Negotiable
Every H1B business acquisition that involves an SBA loan requires:
1. Immigration attorney review of the transaction structure
2. Written opinion that the structure is H1B-compliant
3. Operating agreement that explicitly reflects the passive investor role
4. The attorney's letter may be requested by the SBA lender as part of underwriting

**This is not optional. It protects the buyer's visa status.**

## Required Conditions for H1B Buyer (Must Be in Place Before Loan Closing)

1. **Hire a qualified GM** — US citizen or permanent resident with genuine operational authority
   - Compensation must be market-rate (see `dscr_calculator.md` for GM salary benchmarks)
   - Should have an employment agreement in place at closing
   - Lender may require the GM's resume and employment agreement as part of the loan package

2. **GM must be a real hire, not a nominee** — SBA underwriters will scrutinize whether the
   GM is a genuine operator. A family member with no industry experience raises red flags.

3. **Operating agreement must be structured correctly**
   - H1B buyer is passive investor / equity holder
   - GM has operational authority spelled out in the agreement
   - No language that requires the H1B buyer to perform operational duties

4. **Immigration attorney letter** — confirming the structure is H1B-compliant and that the
   personal guarantee does not create an employment relationship under H1B terms

5. **Personal guarantee** — required from all owners with >20% stake, including H1B buyer

## Business Verticals — SBA Treatment

### Commercial Cleaning / Janitorial
- **SBA category:** Standard small business — no special complications
- **Approval rate:** High — straightforward, low-liability business model
- **Assets:** Primarily equipment (low tangible asset value); loan often unsecured beyond personal guarantee
- **Key underwriting focus:** Customer concentration (one client = 30%+ of revenue is a risk)
- **H1B note:** Contract-based revenue is stable for DSCR but requires GM who can manage crews and client relationships

### Car Wash
- **SBA category:** Approved — capital-intensive, tangible assets support collateral
- **Approval rate:** High for express/in-bay automatic; lower for full-service (labor-intensive)
- **Assets:** Land, building, equipment — good collateral base for SBA loan
- **Special requirement:** Environmental assessment (Phase I at minimum, possibly Phase II)
  - Car washes use chemicals, have underground systems, and can have soil/groundwater issues
  - Environmental problems can kill or delay a deal significantly
  - Budget $2,000-$5,000 for environmental assessment
- **Best deal types for H1B buyer:** Express car wash (IBA) or self-serve — minimal staff, easy to manage passively
- **H1B note:** Express car wash with on-site manager is ideal passive structure

### Laundromat
- **SBA category:** Approved — coin laundry is SBA-eligible, tangible assets (washers, dryers)
- **Approval rate:** High — well-understood business model with 40+ year SBA history
- **Assets:** Equipment (washers/dryers), leasehold improvements, fixtures
- **Special consideration:** Lease assignability — laundromats are lease-dependent; landlord consent to assignment is critical
- **Best deal types for H1B buyer:** Unattended or minimally-attended — 1 part-time attendant, easy absentee management
- **H1B note:** Coin/card laundromats run largely on auto-pilot; GM can be part-time (lower cost)

## Businesses That Create SBA Complications — Watch For

These are NOT automatic disqualifiers but require extra scrutiny:

- **Owner-operator with no staff** — if value is entirely tied to one person's relationships or labor,
  SBA lender will question whether the business survives ownership transfer
- **Large undocumented cash revenue** — SBA lenders use only tax-return revenue; if stated SDE relies
  heavily on add-backs or cash that doesn't show in returns, the DSCR will be lower on paper
- **Short history (under 3 years)** — SBA typically requires 3 years of business tax returns; shorter
  history requires a stronger story and possibly more equity injection
- **Declining revenue trend** — a business showing 3 years of declining revenue faces tough underwriting
  regardless of current SDE; lenders will project forward using the trend
- **Troubled lease** — short lease remaining, no renewal options, or a landlord known to be difficult
  with assignments can kill an SBA deal in underwriting
- **Franchises** — buyer excludes franchises; FDD review adds 30-60 days and SBA must approve the
  franchise concept separately (already filtered out by buyer criteria)

## Approved SBA Lenders — Southeast Region

### Preferred Lender Program (PLP) Lenders
PLP lenders have delegated authority to approve SBA loans without full SBA review, significantly
shortening timelines. Always prefer PLP lenders.

### Live Oak Bank (HIGHEST PRIORITY for Car Wash and Laundromat)
- **Specialization:** Explicitly specializes in laundromat and car wash SBA loans nationally
- **Why they matter:** They have industry-specific underwriters who understand these business models
  — they won't ask you to explain what a card laundromat is or whether car washes are SBA-eligible
- **Typical timeline:** 45-75 days (faster than average due to industry focus)
- **Contact approach:** Have your deal package ready before first call; they move fast
- **Website:** liveoakbank.com/industries/car-washes / liveoakbank.com/industries/laundromats

### Harvest Small Business Finance
- **Specialization:** Service businesses, acquisitions in Southeast US
- **Why they matter:** PLP lender with active Southeast presence
- **Typical timeline:** 60-90 days

### Byline Bank
- **Specialization:** SBA 7(a) and 504 nationally, service business acquisitions
- **Why they matter:** Aggressive on H1B deals when structure is clean
- **Typical timeline:** 60-90 days

### Local Community Banks with SBA Departments
- Often overlooked but can be competitive on smaller deals ($500K-$1M loan amounts)
- Relationship lending can compensate for marginal DSCR if buyer has strong financial profile
- Ask: "Are you a PLP lender?" — if no, add 30-45 days to the timeline

### Note on Multiple Lender Applications
It is acceptable and often advisable to approach 2-3 SBA lenders simultaneously. Each will
provide a preliminary indication before requiring a formal application. Compare:
- Rate quoted
- Equity injection required (some lenders require 15-20% even when SBA minimum is 10%)
- Timeline estimate
- Whether they have experience with this business type specifically

## Common Misunderstandings About H1B and SBA Loans

**Myth: "H1B holders can't get SBA loans."**
False. H1B holders can and do receive SBA 7(a) loans as passive investors.

**Myth: "I need a green card to buy a business."**
False. Business ownership is not employment. No work authorization is needed to own equity.

**Myth: "The GM has to own part of the business."**
Not required. The GM can be a salaried employee with no equity stake.

**Myth: "Giving a personal guarantee will affect my visa."**
A personal guarantee is a financial obligation, not employment. Most immigration attorneys
agree this does not create an H1B employment relationship. Get written confirmation.

**Myth: "I just need any US citizen to sign as the operator."**
No. SBA underwriters will scrutinize whether the named operator is a genuine manager with
actual authority and industry knowledge. A nominee who is clearly unqualified raises fraud
concerns. The GM must be a real hire.
