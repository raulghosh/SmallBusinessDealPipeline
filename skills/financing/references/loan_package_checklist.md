# SBA 7(a) Loan Package Checklist

## Overview

An SBA 7(a) loan for a business acquisition requires a substantial document package. Missing or
late documents are the #1 cause of timeline delays. This checklist is organized by phase so the
buyer can start collecting documents now, before the loan application is even submitted.

**H1B buyers have additional required documents beyond the standard list.** These are marked
with [H1B] throughout.

**Timeline expectation:** Application to close typically takes 60-120 days. Starting document
collection early compresses this timeline.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-LOI Documents (Gather Now)

These documents relate to the buyer's personal financial profile. Start collecting them
immediately — they don't depend on which deal you're pursuing.

### Personal Financial Documents
- [ ] **SBA Form 413 — Personal Financial Statement**
  - Complete and sign (available at sba.gov)
  - Must be dated within 90 days of loan application
  - Include all assets, liabilities, and income sources

- [ ] **Personal Tax Returns — 3 Most Recent Years**
  - Full federal returns including all schedules
  - If married, include spouse's returns if applicable
  - If self-employed or business owner, include Schedule C/K-1

- [ ] **Personal Credit Authorization**
  - Lender will provide their own form
  - Authorizes hard credit pull
  - 720+ credit score will support the application

- [ ] **Buyer Resume / Bio**
  - Emphasize relevant industry experience
  - Management experience is important
  - If no direct industry experience, emphasize transferable skills (operations, management, finance)
  - 1-2 pages maximum

- [ ] **Business Plan / Acquisition Rationale**
  - 1-2 page narrative explaining why you're buying this business
  - Brief overview of how you plan to operate it (passive structure — explain GM model)
  - Why you believe the business will continue to perform post-acquisition
  - Does NOT need to be a full 20-page business plan for acquisition financing

### [H1B] Additional Phase 1 Documents
- [ ] **Copy of H1B Visa + Approval Notice (Form I-797)**
  - Current approval notice
  - If in extension period, include both original and extension approvals

- [ ] **Passport Copy**
  - All pages with stamps if lender requests
  - Minimum: biographical page + visa page

- [ ] **USCIS Work Authorization Documentation**
  - I-797 approval notice
  - If H1B extension pending, document of continued work authorization

- [ ] **Immigration Attorney Engagement**
  - Engage immigration attorney early — their review and letter will be needed in Phase 3
  - Budget $2,000-$5,000 for immigration attorney review of transaction structure
  - Ask specifically: "Is a personal guarantee on an SBA loan H1B-compliant in my situation?"

## Phase 2: With LOI (Submit with Loan Application)

These documents depend on which deal you're pursuing. Collect them once you have a signed LOI.

### Deal Documents
- [ ] **Signed LOI (Letter of Intent)**
  - Must include: purchase price, terms, financing contingency, due diligence period
  - SBA application cannot proceed without a signed LOI
  - Financing contingency protects buyer if SBA loan isn't approved

- [ ] **Proposed Ownership Structure / Organizational Documents**
  - How the entity will be structured (LLC, S-Corp, etc.)
  - Ownership percentages
  - Operating agreement draft (must reflect passive investor role for H1B buyer)

### Business Financial Documents (From Seller / Broker)
- [ ] **Business Tax Returns — 3 Most Recent Years**
  - Federal business tax returns (Form 1120, 1120S, or 1065)
  - These are the primary source lenders use for DSCR — not broker-provided financials
  - Missing or incomplete returns is a major red flag

- [ ] **Business P&L Statements — 3 Years + YTD**
  - Accountant-prepared or CPA-reviewed preferred
  - YTD within 90 days of application

- [ ] **Business Balance Sheet — Most Recent**
  - Prepared by accountant
  - Must match tax returns (discrepancies require explanation)

- [ ] **Accounts Receivable / Payable Aging**
  - 90-day aging report
  - Identifies collection problems and vendor payment issues

- [ ] **Equipment List with Values**
  - All major equipment, age, condition, and estimated current value
  - For car wash: equipment list is critical collateral
  - For laundromat: list of all washers/dryers, age, brand, condition

- [ ] **Lease Agreement (or Landlord Term Sheet)**
  - Current lease with all amendments
  - Must be assignable (or landlord consent to assignment confirmed)
  - Remaining term: SBA lenders typically want remaining lease term ≥ loan term
  - Option periods: confirm option terms are favorable

- [ ] **Customer List / Contract Summary**
  - For janitorial: list of current contracts, contract length, renewal dates, revenue per client
  - Customer concentration analysis (no single client >30% of revenue ideally)
  - For car wash/laundromat: not required (retail customer base)

### [H1B] Critical Phase 2 Document
- [ ] **GM Identified and Employment Offer Letter (or Letter of Intent to Hire)**
  - **This is not optional for an H1B buyer — it is a financing prerequisite**
  - GM must be US citizen or permanent resident
  - Offer letter or employment agreement must be ready at application
  - GM's resume should be included (lender will evaluate GM's qualifications)
  - If GM not yet identified, loan application should not be submitted

## Phase 3: Underwriting (Provided When Lender Requests)

The lender will provide a due diligence checklist. These are the items commonly requested.

### Third-Party Reports
- [ ] **Business Valuation Report**
  - Required by SBA for any acquisition loan over $250,000
  - Must be prepared by a qualified business appraiser (NACVA, ASA, or similar credential)
  - Cost: $2,500-$5,000 typically
  - Timeline: 2-3 weeks — order early
  - Valuation must support the purchase price (or explain variance)

- [ ] **Environmental Assessment (Phase I)**
  - **Required for car wash deals — mandatory, not optional**
  - Recommended for any property with underground storage tanks, chemical use, or industrial history
  - Cost: $2,000-$3,500 for Phase I
  - If Phase I identifies concerns: Phase II site assessment required ($10,000-$50,000+)
  - Car wash deal buyers: budget for this early; environmental issues can kill deals

- [ ] **Real Estate Appraisal**
  - Required if real estate is included in the purchase price
  - Must be performed by licensed appraiser
  - Cost: $3,000-$5,000
  - For leased properties: not required, but lease assignability letter from landlord may be needed

### SBA-Specific Forms
- [ ] **SBA Form 1919 — Borrower Information Form**
  - Background questions for all owners and key management
  - Includes criminal history disclosure
  - Must be completed by buyer and by GM (Phase 2 hire)

- [ ] **SBA Form 912 — Statement of Personal History**
  - Required if any criminal history is disclosed on Form 1919

### [H1B] Required Phase 3 Documents
- [ ] **Immigration Attorney Opinion Letter**
  - Written letter from qualified immigration attorney
  - Confirms that passive investment structure and personal guarantee are H1B-compliant
  - Confirms that the H1B holder will not be performing duties that constitute employment
  - Lender underwriting team and SBA may request this — have it ready
  - Budget $2,000-$3,000 for the opinion letter specifically

- [ ] **Operating Agreement — Final Version**
  - Must explicitly state that H1B holder is a passive investor
  - Must define GM's operational authority
  - Must not contain language requiring H1B holder to perform operational duties
  - Attorney should review for both corporate and immigration compliance

- [ ] **GM Employment Agreement — Executed**
  - Final executed employment agreement with the US citizen/permanent resident GM
  - Must be in place before loan closing
  - Should include: compensation, title, authority, duties, non-compete (to protect buyer)

## Timeline Expectations

| Milestone | Typical Timeline |
|-----------|----------------|
| Document collection starts | Now (Phase 1 immediately) |
| LOI signed | Day 0 (triggers Phase 2) |
| Loan application submitted | Day 7-14 after LOI (allow time to collect Phase 2 docs) |
| Application to conditional approval | 30-60 days |
| Conditional approval to final approval | 15-30 days |
| Final approval to closing | 15-30 days |
| **Total: LOI to closing** | **60-120 days** |

### Live Oak Bank (Car Wash / Laundromat Specialist)
- Industry expertise accelerates underwriting
- Often achieves 45-75 days from application to close
- Their underwriters know laundromat and car wash models — less back-and-forth on basics

### Tips to Accelerate Timeline
1. Start Phase 1 document collection now, before selecting a deal
2. When submitting LOI, request all financial documents immediately — don't wait
3. Order business valuation and environmental assessment (if car wash) as soon as LOI is signed
4. Engage immigration attorney now — the opinion letter takes 2-3 weeks minimum
5. Identify GM candidate early — lenders can't finalize approval without GM in place for H1B buyer

## Red Flags That Delay or Kill SBA Approval

The following issues commonly cause loan declines or multi-month delays. Flag these early:

**Financial Documentation Issues**
- Seller cannot produce 3 years of business tax returns
  → Major red flag — raises fraud suspicion, may be unfixable
- Large discrepancy between tax returns and broker-stated SDE
  → Common (brokers use add-backs, lenders use tax returns); document and explain each add-back
- Revenue decline trend over 3 years
  → Lender projects forward using trend; declining revenue can kill approval regardless of current SDE

**Property / Lease Issues**
- Lease not assignable without landlord consent
  → Very common; get landlord consent letter early; some landlords use this to raise rent
- Short lease remaining (under 5 years with no options)
  → SBA lender wants lease term ≥ loan term; short lease may require shorter loan or deal restructure
- Environmental issues at car wash
  → Phase I / Phase II findings can add months and significant cost; worst case: deal killed

**Operational Issues**
- No qualified GM identified (H1B buyer)
  → Cannot close without this; do not submit application without GM identified and offer extended
- Owner is the sole operator with all client relationships
  → Creates key-person risk; lender will question whether business survives ownership transfer

**Title / Legal Issues**
- Title defects on business assets or real estate
- Pending litigation against the business
- UCC liens on equipment that seller hasn't addressed
- Unpaid payroll taxes or IRS liens (subordinate to SBA — must be resolved at closing)

## H1B-Specific Required Documents Summary

Quick reference list of all H1B-specific documents needed, in order:

**Phase 1 (Gather Now):**
1. H1B visa + I-797 approval notice
2. Passport copy
3. Engage immigration attorney

**Phase 2 (With LOI):**
4. GM identified — US citizen or permanent resident
5. GM offer letter or employment agreement
6. GM resume
7. Operating agreement draft reflecting passive investor role

**Phase 3 (Underwriting):**
8. Immigration attorney opinion letter (H1B compliance confirmation)
9. Operating agreement — final executed version
10. GM employment agreement — fully executed
11. SBA Form 1919 completed by both buyer and GM

**Total H1B-specific budget add-on:** $4,000-$8,000 (immigration attorney fees, additional
legal review of operating agreement structure)
