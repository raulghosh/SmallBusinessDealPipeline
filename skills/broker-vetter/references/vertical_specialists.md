# Vertical Specialist Identification Guide

How to identify brokers who genuinely specialize in janitorial, car wash, or laundromat deals (vs. generalists who list everything).

---

## Core Principle

**Specialization ≠ self-claimed.** A broker listing "we sell all business types including janitorial, car washes, and laundromats" is a generalist with SEO. A broker with 8 closed laundromat deals and a vertical-specific intake process is a specialist.

Verify specialization through **deal evidence**, not vertical pages.

---

## JANITORIAL / COMMERCIAL CLEANING SPECIALISTS

### Signals of genuine specialization
- Case studies specifically mention:
  - Commercial contract portfolios (not residential)
  - Customer concentration analysis (did they verify that no single client was >20% of revenue?)
  - W-2 vs 1099 workforce audit
  - Recurring contract book value vs spot work
- Broker attends or presents at ISSA (International Sanitary Supply Association) or BSCAI (Building Service Contractors Association International) events
- Deal size mix includes multi-site janitorial (suggests they understand operational scale)
- Mentions of route density, crew leader retention, client retention rate in case studies
- BSCAI membership or sponsorship

### Red flags for fake specialization
- Lists "janitorial" alongside "restaurants, retail, service businesses" — generalist tell
- Uses the term "cleaning service" without distinguishing residential vs commercial
- No mention of contract portfolio analysis or customer concentration
- Case studies reference residential cleaning (different business model entirely)

### Deal-specific evaluation questions
1. What % of their closed deals are commercial janitorial specifically?
2. Can they share anonymized examples of contract portfolio analysis they've done?
3. Do they understand how to value recurring contracts vs one-time work?

## CAR WASH SPECIALISTS

### Signals of genuine specialization
- Distinguishes between **express/tunnel**, **self-serve**, **flex-serve**, and **full-service** in case studies
- Mentions equipment-specific language: belt vs chain conveyor, soft-touch vs touchless, tunnel length, reclaim water systems
- Understands real estate vs business split (often the real estate is 50-70% of total deal value)
- Mentions ICA (International Carwash Association) attendance or membership
- Case studies include operational metrics: monthly member count, capture rate, average ticket, cars per day
- Knows equipment lifespan implications (tunnel equipment 10-15 year replacement cycle)

### Red flags for fake specialization
- Treats car wash as "location-based service business" without model distinction
- Can't explain membership revenue vs transactional revenue difference
- No case studies separating real estate from business value
- Doesn't mention equipment age or capex cycle
- Uses generic "automotive services" bucket

### Deal-specific evaluation questions
1. What models have they sold (express tunnel vs self-serve vs full-service)?
2. How do they handle real estate separation in valuation?
3. Can they explain the capex cycle on tunnel equipment?
4. What's their view on membership program valuation premiums?

---

## LAUNDROMAT SPECIALISTS

### Signals of genuine specialization
- Distinguishes **unattended self-service**, **attended**, **WDF (wash-dry-fold)**, **pickup/delivery**
- Understands coin vs card/app payment implications (revenue verifiability)
- Case studies reference:
  - Turns per day / turns per machine
  - Lease term vs equipment payback period
  - Utility expense ratios (water + gas typically 20-30% of revenue)
  - Equipment age and replacement economics
- CLA (Coin Laundry Association) membership or conference attendance
- Mentions specific equipment brands (Speed Queen, Dexter, Huebsch, Milnor, Continental Girbau)

### Red flags for fake specialization
- Treats laundromat as "simple coin-operated business"
- Doesn't mention revenue verification challenges with coin-only operations
- No discussion of lease term as core deal constraint
- Missing utility cost analysis
- No equipment vintage discussion

### Deal-specific evaluation questions
1. How do they verify revenue on coin-only laundromats?
2. What's their view on lease term requirements?
3. How do they evaluate equipment age and replacement timing?
4. Do they understand the unattended-vs-attended operational difference?

---

## CROSS-VERTICAL SIGNALS OF QUALITY

Regardless of vertical, look for brokers who:

### Quality of thought
- Ask you questions back (about your goals, timeline, capital) vs pitching immediately
- Differentiate between "business value" and "real estate value"
- Can explain SDE vs EBITDA appropriately
- Understand multi-year trend analysis (not just TTM)

### Process discipline
- Use NDAs before sharing financials
- Require proof of funds before deep dives
- Qualify buyers before scheduling seller calls
- Have a documented diligence process

### Specialization depth
- Know industry-specific seller motivations (retirement, burnout, capex cliff)
- Understand vertical-specific red flags
- Can reference industry benchmarks from memory (valuation multiples, margin expectations)
- Have opinions about which brands/equipment/models are better investments

## HOW TO SURFACE SPECIALISTS IN DISCOVERY

When pulling candidates from directories:

1. **IBBA/M&A Source:** Look for "Industry Expertise" filter if available. Search for "cleaning", "car wash", "laundry", "laundromat", "janitorial".

2. **BizBuySell profiles:** Filter by active listings. A broker with 3+ listings in your target vertical is a specialist. One listing is coincidence.

3. **Case study search:** Google `site:[broker-domain.com] "laundromat" case study` to find vertical-specific content.

4. **Trade association member lists:**
   - BSCAI member directory: https://www.bscai.org (janitorial)
   - ICA member directory: https://www.carwash.org (car wash)
   - CLA member directory: https://www.coinlaundry.org (laundromat)
   - Cross-reference these with your broker candidate list.

5. **LinkedIn search:** Search for "business broker" + "laundromat" OR "car wash" OR "janitorial" + [state]. Brokers with vertical keywords in titles/headlines are signaling specialization.

## SPECIALIZATION SCORING OVERLAY

Use this on top of the base scoring rubric:

**+2 additional points** if broker has:
- ≥3 closed deals in your target vertical (with verifiable case studies), AND
- Active membership in vertical trade association (BSCAI/ICA/CLA)

**+1 additional point** if broker has:
- ≥1 closed deal in target vertical, AND
- Demonstrated knowledge via blog posts or conference talks

**0 additional points** (no boost) if broker:
- Lists the vertical on a "we sell these" page without deal evidence

**-2 penalty** if broker:
- Claims specialization but can't name a single vertical-specific deal
- Confuses vertical sub-types (treats express tunnel like full-service, or unattended like WDF)
