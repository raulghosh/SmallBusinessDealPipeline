# Broker Scoring Rubric

## Core Philosophy
- **Evidence-weighted**: Claims without verifiable sources score 0
- **Conservative**: Under-include quality brokers rather than include lemons
- **Disqualifiers are absolute**: One red flag → OUT (no point balancing)

---

## Scoring Scale: 0-20 points maximum

A broker MUST clear disqualifier checks FIRST. Only then do they get scored on positive signals.

### Tier Cutoffs
- **Tier 1 (Trusted):** 16-20 points
- **Tier 2 (Qualified):** 12-15 points
- **Borderline (Human review):** 10-13 points (overlap with Tier 2 is intentional)
- **Rejected:** <10 points
- **Disqualified:** Any red flag triggered, regardless of score

---

## HARD SIGNALS (+3 points each, max +12)

Each requires **verifiable external evidence** (URL). Self-claims don't count.

### 1. Industry Membership (+3)
Must be verified on the **membership org's own website**, not the broker's:
- IBBA (International Business Brokers Association) — check ibba.org directory
- M&A Source — check masource.org directory
- GABB (Georgia Association of Business Brokers) — check gabb.org directory
- State-level BBA (e.g., Florida Business Brokers Association)

**Evidence required:** URL to the membership org's directory page listing this broker.

### 2. Named Brokers with Verifiable Track Record (+3)
The firm lists specific humans (not "our team") and those humans have:
- LinkedIn profiles (check account age + connection count)
- Bio mentions specific deals closed OR specific tenure (>5 years)
- Photos match LinkedIn (not stock images)

**Evidence required:** Links to 2+ team members' LinkedIn profiles or verifiable bios.

### 3. Public Closed-Deal Case Studies (+3)
Not testimonials. Actual case studies with:
- Business name OR vertical + approximate size + year
- Multi-paragraph writeup
- Specific outcome (sale price, timeline, buyer type)

A testimonial like "Great broker!" is NOT a case study. A page titled "We recently closed a $1.2M laundromat sale in Decatur GA in 2023" IS.

**Evidence required:** URL to case study page. Count ≥3 distinct case studies.

### 4. Years Active (>10 years) (+3)
Verified via **state licensing record**, not broker's own site claim.

**Evidence required:** State broker license registry URL showing registration date.

---

## MEDIUM SIGNALS (+2 points each, max +8)

### 1. BizBuySell / BizQuest Verified Presence (+2)
- Active listings on BizBuySell or BizQuest (not just a claim page)
- "Verified Broker" badge if the platform offers one
- Active listings consistently over time (not a one-off)

**Evidence required:** URL to broker's profile on the platform + ≥3 active or recently closed listings.

### 2. Repeat Legitimate Awards (+2)
Awards from credible orgs (NOT SEO award mills):
- GABB Multi-Million Dollar Club
- IBBA Deal Maker of the Year
- State broker association awards

**NOT counted:**
- "Top broker 2023" from generic directory sites
- "Best business broker" from paid listing services
- Self-published awards

**Evidence required:** URL on the awarding org's website.

### 3. Vertical Specialization Match (+2)
Explicit mention of janitorial/car wash/laundromat in:
- Firm's specialty pages
- Past deals (listed in case studies or BizBuySell history)
- Broker bio

**Evidence required:** URL to page mentioning the specific vertical.

### 4. Active Industry Engagement (+2)
- Presents at IBBA conferences / publishes articles in trade journals
- Teaches CBI (Certified Business Intermediary) coursework
- Regularly updates blog with deal-specific content (not SEO filler)

**Evidence required:** URL to speaking engagement, article, or CBI certification.

---

## DISQUALIFIERS (Automatic rejection, no exceptions)

Apply these FIRST. If any is triggered, broker is OUT regardless of positive signals.

### Red Flag 1: Website Broken or Outdated
- Any deal-related page returns 404
- "Latest news" or blog not updated in 18+ months
- Copyright footer still says 2021 or earlier
- Mobile version broken
- SSL certificate invalid

**Rationale:** A broker who can't maintain their own website cannot be trusted to manage a 6-month deal process.

### Red Flag 2: Suspicious Review Patterns
Trigger if ANY of:
- 100% 5-star reviews with <25 total reviews
- >50% of reviews posted within a 30-day window
- Multiple reviews using identical phrasing
- Reviews reference services/cities the broker doesn't actually serve
- Reviewer accounts have only 1 review each

**Rationale:** Review farming is a learned behavior. If they game reviews, they'll game representations in deals.

### Red Flag 3: No Named Brokers / Anonymous Ops
- Website shows "Our Team" with no individual names
- Team photos are stock images (reverse image search positive)
- LinkedIn company page has no employees listed
- Phone number is Google Voice / VOIP with no office address
- Office address is a mailbox service (UPS Store, etc.)

**Rationale:** Legitimate brokers publicize their team. Anonymity is a churn-and-burn signal.

### Red Flag 4: "#1 Broker" Language Without Proof
Trigger if website uses language like:
- "#1 business broker in [state]"
- "Best-rated broker"
- "America's top business broker"
- "Trusted by thousands"

WITHOUT citing the source of the ranking.

**Rationale:** Unsubstantiated superlatives = marketing over substance.

### Red Flag 5: No Deal Transparency
Trigger if:
- Zero case studies or deal history pages
- No testimonials, OR testimonials all anonymized ("J.S., business owner")
- Refuses to provide references when asked
- Won't name any past clients even with permission

**Rationale:** You cannot verify track record without transparency. Opacity is hiding something.

### Red Flag 6: Registered in Wrong State / Unlicensed
- Broker operates in state X but only licensed in state Y
- License status "inactive" or "suspended"
- No license record found in state registry

**Rationale:** Some states don't require broker licenses, but if your state does (GA does NOT require a separate license, but many states do), check.

**Note:** Georgia does NOT require a separate business broker license. Real estate brokers in GA can broker business sales under their real estate license. This is a legal nuance, not a disqualifier.

### Red Flag 7: Fee Structure Opaque or Predatory
- Refuses to share fee structure without engagement
- Charges "upfront retainer" >$5K without deliverables
- Success fee >12% on deals <$2M (industry norm is 8-12%)
- Charges buy-side AND sell-side on same deal (conflict of interest)

**Rationale:** If the fee structure is hidden, the broker is hiding something.

## SCORING WORKED EXAMPLE

**Broker: Atlanta Business Advisors**

**Disqualifier Check:**
- Website updated last month ✓
- 47 Google reviews, 4.6 avg, spread over 4 years ✓
- 5 named team members with LinkedIn ✓
- No "#1" language ✓
- 8 case studies with details ✓
- GA, no license required ✓
- Fee structure published on FAQ page ✓

**No disqualifiers triggered. Proceed to scoring.**

**Hard Signals (+3 each):**
- GABB member (verified at gabb.org/directory): +3
- Named brokers with track records (3 LinkedIn verified): +3
- 8 public case studies with specifics: +3
- 18 years active per GA corp registry: +3
- **Hard subtotal: 12/12**

**Medium Signals (+2 each):**
- 12 active BizBuySell listings: +2
- GABB Multi-Million Dollar Club (2021, 2022, 2023): +2
- 2 laundromat case studies + 1 janitorial case study: +2
- Managing partner teaches CBI course: +2
- **Medium subtotal: 8/8**

**Total Score: 20/20 → Tier 1 (Trusted)**

## ANTI-PATTERN WORKED EXAMPLE

**Broker: Premier Southeast Business Sales**

**Disqualifier Check:**
- Website last updated 2022 (per footer) ❌ **DISQUALIFIER: Website outdated**

**STOP. Do not score positive signals.**

**Verdict: DISQUALIFIED — Website outdated**

Even if this broker would have scored 18/20 on positive signals, they're out.

## Calibration Notes

Over time, track false positive / false negative rates:
- **False positive:** Scored high, turned out to be a bad broker (adjust rubric UP)
- **False negative:** Disqualified, but user talked to them and they were fine (adjust rubric DOWN)

Record calibration notes in `skills/broker-vetter/calibration_log.md` as you accumulate experience.
